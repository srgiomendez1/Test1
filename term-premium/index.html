How it works · performed weekly

Five steps from dealer screens to an approved term premium

Situation: our FTP curve prices every product off FTP(t) = SOFR(t) + TP(t), so the term premium schedule is our marginal cost of unsecured term funding. Complication: that premium isn't directly observable — it must be extracted weekly from where banks like us actually buy money. Question: does this week's market read justify changing the approved schedule? The five steps below produce the answer.

market_TP(t) = max( mean7 issuers[ CD_offer(t) ], own_issuance(t) ) − SOFR(t)
decision: |market_TP(t) − approved_TP(t)| > 20bp → committee discussion; 13-week trend distinguishes persistent repricing from noise

Standing reference · methodology & governance

Why the curve is built this way

This section is the durable record: the rationale for each design choice, the questions worth pressure-testing in committee, and the decision log. It complements the Interpolation Methodology document (FTP curve construction) and the MDD.

Why brokered CDs?

They are our observable marginal unsecured price. FTP best practice prices funds at today's marginal cost of raising them from non-customer sources — the "buy" side of buy-vs-make. Brokered CDs are the deepest such market a regional bank taps weekly, with posted, executable levels across a full term ladder. Post-2008 consensus (BIS liquidity transfer pricing guidance) is a direct pass-through of the market cost of liquidity — not an internally modeled one.

Why average 7 issuers?

One name is an anecdote; a panel is a market. Averaging across issuers of comparable standing removes single-issuer credit idiosyncrasy and week-to-week posting gaps. The panel's composition is itself a governance choice: it should look like us (size, rating, business mix), and membership changes should be documented — swapping a tight G-SIB for a wide consumer lender moves the curve without any market move.

Why floor at our own prints?

Our executed cost is the truth; the panel is the estimate. When we actually issued at a term, that level is realized marginal cost — if it exceeds the panel average, using the average would understate the cost we demonstrably pay (adverse selection: we get done where our name clears). The floor only lifts, never lowers: deliberate conservatism that keeps FTP from subsidizing lending off a curve we cannot fund at.

Why subtract SOFR?

To isolate what is ours from what is the Fed's. The same-term SOFR OIS rate is the market's near-risk-free expectation of policy over the term. Subtracting it strips rate expectations out of the CD level; the residual — term liquidity + unsecured credit premium — is the piece ALCO actually governs, and the piece that plugs into FTP(t) = SOFR(t) + TP(t).

Why show FHLB?

It prices the collateral, and disciplines the level. FHLB advances are our secured alternative; the CD−FHLB gap is the market price of borrowing unsecured (our name risk), while FHLB−SOFR is mostly the term/structure premium of secured wholesale. If the unsecured premium collapses toward FHLB, the market is giving liquidity away; if it gaps wide, our name is repricing — different conversations, same chart.

Why a ±20bp indifference range?

Stability is a feature of a cost-of-funds curve. Product pricing, profitability reporting and incentive comp all key off FTP; chasing every 5bp wiggle churns all three. 20bp is the materiality threshold at which repricing risk outweighs stability — combined with the 13-week trend test, we only move for changes that are both material and persistent. The band itself should be re-validated periodically against realized premium volatility.

Pressure-test questions before committee (known gaps)

  • Quote convention. Are all issuer levels the same convention (APY vs bond-equivalent, monthly vs semiannual pay, actual/365 vs 30/360)? A convention mismatch of 3–6bp can eat a third of the indifference band silently.
  • Callable structures. Brokered ladders mix bullet and callable paper at 2Y+; a callable level is not comparable to a bullet. Confirm the panel is bullet-only or document the adjustment.
  • Executable vs posted. Top-of-screen posts are advertisements; fills happen deeper. Where possible anchor to executed levels (our own prints do this at floored terms — elsewhere it's an acknowledged approximation).
  • Thin long end. At 4Y–5Y few issuers post (this week's sample: 3–5 quotes). The average is noisier exactly where the premium is largest — consider requiring a minimum quote count or carrying a wider band at those terms.
  • Panel survivorship. An issuer that pulls back from a term when funding gets expensive removes the widest quote — the average falls for the wrong reason. Track posting gaps, not just levels.
  • Floor asymmetry. The floor lifts but never lowers — over time this embeds a conservative bias. That's intended; quantify it annually (average floor lift, bp) so the bias is chosen, not accidental.
  • Cadence mismatch. Observation is weekly, the approved schedule updates monthly (ALMC). Document how mid-cycle breaches are handled: escalate immediately, or hold to cycle unless the breach exceeds a hard limit.
  • Contingent-liquidity credit. The retail CD product earns FTP − 10bp for its liquidity value; that credit is separate from this market premium and shouldn't be conflated when explaining CD pricing.

Decision log

Glossary

Term premium — TP(t)
The spread over same-term SOFR that the unsecured wholesale market charges us for term money: term liquidity premium + our unsecured credit spread. The ALMC-approved TP schedule is added to SOFR to form the FTP curve.
Brokered CD
A certificate of deposit distributed through broker-dealers to retail investors; for the issuing bank it behaves like unsecured wholesale term funding with a posted primary-market rate ladder.
SOFR / SOFR OIS
Secured Overnight Financing Rate; the OIS swap of a given term is the market's average expected SOFR over that term — our proxy for the risk-free base at each tenor (Term SOFR 1–6M, OIS swaps 1Y+).
FHLB advance
Collateralized borrowing from the Federal Home Loan Bank. Secured, so it prices near the risk-free curve — the reference for what our funding costs with collateral pledged.
Indifference range
The ±bp corridor around the approved schedule within which observed market premiums trigger no action. Materiality threshold for repricing the FTP curve.
Floored average
max(panel average, own executed issuance) at each term — the conservative estimate of our marginal unsecured cost.
