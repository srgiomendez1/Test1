# Foods to Avoid — Sergio's DNA Protocol

> Every item on this list is tied to at least one specific genetic variant. This is not a general diet recommendation — these are your personal inflammatory triggers.

---

## Absolute Avoidances (Zero Tolerance)

### Cow Dairy — MCM6 +/+ (rs4988235 G/G · rs182549 C/C)
You are homozygous lactase non-persistent and cow casein-A1 intolerant.

| ❌ Avoid | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Cow milk (whole, skim, 2%) | Casein A1 + lactose → gut inflammation, leaky gut |
| Cow cheese (all types) | Even aged/hard cheese; casein triggers immune response |
| Whey protein (cow-derived) | Common in protein powders, bars; same inflammatory pathway |
| Cow yogurt / kefir | Fermentation reduces lactose but not casein |
| Butter (from cow) | Small amounts of ghee are fine (pure fat, no protein) |
| Ice cream | Sugar + dairy double-hit |
| Casein protein powder | Direct casein source |

**Alternatives:** Goat or sheep dairy (test individually), ghee, plant-based milks (unsweetened), coconut yogurt

---

### Seed Oils — PON1 +/+ (rs854560 A/A) + GSTP1 +/+
Your reduced PON1 activity means oxidized lipids from seed oils are NOT neutralized and cause direct arterial and gut damage.

| ❌ Avoid | Common Hiding Places |
|----------|---------------------|
| Canola oil | "Vegetable oil," restaurant cooking, most salad dressings |
| Soybean oil | Mayonnaise, margarine, most processed foods |
| Sunflower oil | Chips, crackers, granola bars |
| Corn oil | Popcorn, processed snacks |
| Cottonseed oil | Fast food fryers |
| Grapeseed oil | "Healthy" restaurant options, pesto |
| Rice bran oil | Asian restaurant cooking |
| "Vegetable oil" (any blend) | Virtually everywhere in packaged food |

**Alternatives:** Extra virgin olive oil (cold use + light heat), avocado oil (high heat), ghee or tallow (high heat), coconut oil

**Restaurant rule:** Ask "What oil do you cook in?" and request butter, ghee, or olive oil if available. Avoid anything deep-fried in restaurants unless you can confirm the oil.

## Avoid / Minimize (Significant Impact)

### Excess Sugar and High-Glycemic Carbohydrates
*Triggers:* HLA RA variants (+/-), CDKN2 cardiovascular (+/+), CDKAL1 leaky gut (+/+), CLU Alzheimer's (+/+)

| Minimize | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| White bread, bagels, pastries | Spike blood sugar → inflammation → HLA joint attacks |
| Sugary drinks (juice, soda, energy drinks) | Direct blood sugar spike |
| Sweetened yogurt | Sugar + dairy double hit |
| White rice (large portions) | High glycemic; limit to small portions |
| Candy, desserts | Obvious; occasional exceptions OK if blood sugar is well-controlled |

**Target:** Fasted glucose < 85 mg/dL. Test occasionally with a glucose meter.

---

### Alcohol
*Triggers:* MTHFR methylation, CTH homocysteine, SOD2 oxidative stress, gut health (CDKAL1)

- Alcohol directly depletes B-vitamins needed for your methylation pathway
- Raises homocysteine even in people without MTHFR — compounding for you
- Disrupts gut microbiome (CDKAL1 gut variant)
- Disrupts sleep architecture (ABCC9 sleep variant)
- **If drinking:** Red wine in moderation (quercetin/resveratrol content partially compensates); never on an empty stomach; take B-vitamins day before and after

### Large Predatory Fish (Mercury Load) — GSTP1 +/+
Your reduced heavy metal clearance makes mercury accumulation especially harmful.

| Limit | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Swordfish | Limit to < 1x/month or avoid |
| King mackerel | High mercury; avoid |
| Tilefish | High mercury; avoid |
| Bigeye/ahi tuna | Limit to 2x/month |
| Shark | Avoid |
| Albacore (canned tuna) | Limit to 2x/week max |

**Safe fish:** Sardines, Atlantic salmon (wild-caught), anchovies, herring, rainbow trout, tilapia — low mercury, high omega-3

## Foods to Be Cautious With (Genetics-Specific Nuance)

| Food | Why | Guidance |
|------|-----|----------|
| Nightshades (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant) | May trigger leaky gut in CDKAL1 variants; individual variation | Test 30-day elimination if joints/gut are problematic |
| Raw spinach/greens (large quantities) | Oxalates compete with mineral absorption (calcium, zinc, magnesium) | Steaming reduces oxalates; cooked is better |
| Legumes/beans | Lectins may worsen leaky gut (CDKAL1) | Pressure-cook to denature lectins; or sprout |
| High-fructose foods | Fructose is metabolized differently; can raise uric acid → joint risk | Limit; fruit in moderation is fine if blood sugar controlled |
