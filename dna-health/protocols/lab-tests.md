## Complete Thyroid Panel (Order Every Component)

Standard doctors often only order TSH. For your TG and DIO2 variants, you need the **full picture**:

| Test | Optimal Target | Why |
|------|---------------|-----|
| TSH | 0.5–1.5 mIU/L | Your risk requires tighter control than standard range (0.4–4.5) |
| Free T4 | Mid-to-upper normal range | Assesses thyroid output |
| Free T3 | Upper quartile of normal | DIO2 impairs T4→T3 conversion; this is the active hormone |
| Reverse T3 (rT3) | < 15 ng/dL | High rT3 blocks T3 receptors; indicates conversion problem |
| TPO antibodies | Negative | Indicates Hashimoto's autoimmune activity |
| TGAb (Thyroglobulin antibodies) | Negative | Specific to TG gene variant; indicates antibody attack |

**If antibodies are positive:** Even with normal TSH, positive antibodies mean autoimmunity is active. Start selenium, optimize gut health, consult integrative medicine physician.

**If Free T3 is low with normal TSH:** DIO2 variant is likely impairing conversion. Ask about T3 supplementation or desiccated thyroid (Armour/NP Thyroid).

## Heavy Metals Test (Optional Baseline)

CYP2A6 +/+ does impair clearance of certain volatile compounds (including some mycotoxins). For a general baseline or if you have known environmental exposures (occupational, dental amalgams, high fish consumption), a metals test is reasonable but not urgent:
- **Collection:** Hair sample (3–4 cm root end) or urine
- **Metals to assess:** Mercury, lead, arsenic, cadmium, aluminum
- **Normal ranges:** Lab-specific; look for anything in red/elevated tier
- **Note:** GSTP1 Val/Val does NOT clearly increase mercury burden — evidence is mixed and one study found Val/Val associated with *lower* blood mercury. Heavy metals testing is a general precaution, not a direct GSTP1 indication.

## How to Request These Tests

**From your primary care physician:**
- Request a "comprehensive metabolic panel" + CBC + lipid panel + iron studies + vitamin D + B12 + folate
- Specifically request: Lp(a), homocysteine, hsCRP, fasting insulin
- For thyroid: request "full thyroid panel including Free T3, Reverse T3, TPO antibodies, and TGAb"

**Direct-to-consumer options (if insurance doesn't cover):**
- Ulta Lab Tests — comprehensive panels at low cost
- LabCorp + Quest via PCP order
- Inside Tracker — good for athletic optimization panel

**Functional medicine physician:**
- For hair heavy metals, comprehensive hormone panels, IV glutathione, and thyroid treatment options
