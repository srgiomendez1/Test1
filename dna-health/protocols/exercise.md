## Non-Negotiable Movements (Bone Density — Wnt16)

These movements produce the mechanical load signals needed to offset your Wnt16 bone density variant:

1. **Deadlift** (conventional or trap bar) — every lower body day; the most evidence-backed movement for spinal and hip bone density
2. **Squat or leg press** — knee-loaded bone stimulus
3. **Overhead press** — upper body + spine loading

Progressive overload: add weight or reps every 2–4 weeks. Bone responds to increasing mechanical stress — the same load for months = no additional stimulus.

## Cardio Protocol (Brain, Heart, Mood)

**Primary mode: Zone 2 (aerobic base)**
- Heart rate: 130–150 BPM (rough guide; can hold a conversation)
- 45–60 minutes, 3–4x/week minimum
- Best options: cycling, rowing machine, brisk walking with incline, swimming

**Why Zone 2 specifically:**
- Triggers mitochondrial biogenesis → compensates for SOD2 oxidative stress
- Drives intracellular autophagy → brain cleanup complementary to CLU extracellular amyloid clearance
- Increases BDNF → brain health and neurogenesis
- Sustained serotonin release → HTR2A/SLC6A4 mood support
- Cardiovascular protection → LPA, CDKN2 risk reduction

**Fasted cardio option:** Your ADIPOQ +/+ means fasted Zone 2 (morning before breaking your fast) maximizes fat burning and metabolic adaptation. Keep sessions under 60 min when fasted; hydrate well.

## Joint Care Protocol (HLA RA · DIO2 · ITGAV)

**Daily movement minimum:**
- Don't sit > 45 minutes without moving; stand, walk, do 5 squats or hip circles
- 10 minute joint mobility circuit daily (morning or evening)

**Joint mobility circuit (10 minutes):**
1. Hip circles — 10 each direction
2. Spinal cat-cow — 10 reps
3. Thoracic rotations — 10 each side
4. Shoulder circles — 10 each direction
5. Wrist and ankle circles — 10 each
6. Knee hugs / hip flexor stretch — 60 sec each
7. Deep squat hold — 30–60 sec

**Infrared light / sauna post-exercise:**
- Increases bloodflow to joint tissues (ITGAV bloodflow variant)
- Reduces inflammatory cytokines (HLA RA risk)
- Reduces Lp(a) risk (LPA variant)
- Target: 20–30 min, 4–5x/week

## Recovery Guidelines

- **Sleep:** 7.5–9 hours; slow-twitch fibers recover faster than fast-twitch but you still need sleep for testosterone synthesis
- **Protein:** 0.7–1.0 g per pound of bodyweight daily; prioritize animal protein for complete amino acid profile
- **Post-workout nutrition:** Protein + carbs within 1–2 hours of training
- **Cold water immersion (optional):** 2–5 min cold shower or cold plunge; increases dopamine/serotonin; reduces joint inflammation. Note: avoid immediately before bed if it disrupts your sleep.

## What to Avoid in Training

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Heavy-impact running as primary cardio | DIO2 + HLA joint risk; use low-impact alternatives |

| Long sedentary periods (even on rest days) | ITGAV bloodflow to joints requires movement |
| NSAIDs (ibuprofen) routinely for exercise soreness | Increases intestinal permeability and worsens gut inflammation risk (CDKAL1 IBD variant) |
