## What to Avoid in Supplements

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| **Folic acid** (synthetic B9) | MTHFR C677T cannot process; competes with methylfolate |
| **Iron** (unless confirmed deficient by labs) | SOD2 +/+ means excess iron causes oxidative damage |
| **Cyanocobalamin (B12)** | Use methylcobalamin instead; cyanocobalamin requires conversion |
| **Calcium carbonate** | Poor absorption; use calcium citrate or food sources |
| **High-dose iodine (> 1,000 mcg)** | Can worsen Hashimoto's in TG variant carriers |

## Suggested Shopping Notes
- **Methylfolate brands:** Thorne 5-MTHF, Seeking Health Optimal Folate, Jarrow 5-MTHF
- **Avoid generic "B-complex" or "multivitamin"** — most contain folic acid + cyanocobalamin; replace with targeted methylated B stack
- **Fish oil quality:** Look for third-party tested (IFOS certification); fresh fish smell = rancid; should be virtually odorless
- **Niacinamide vs Niacin:** Niacinamide (flush-free) for daily use; nicotinic acid (niacin) causes flushing but may have slightly stronger Lp(a) effects — your choice
