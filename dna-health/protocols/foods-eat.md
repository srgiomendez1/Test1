## Your Power Foods (Eat These Regularly)

### Berries — CDKN2A/B + CLU + CDKAL1 + HTR2A
**Eat: ½–1 cup daily**

Berries are your single most important dietary recommendation, appearing across 5 different genetic variants:
- Blueberries: highest anthocyanin content; brain protection (CLU amyloid clearance), anti-inflammatory (CDKAL1 IBD risk)
- Blackberries: high quercetin; cardiovascular (CDKN2A/B ANRIL locus)
- Raspberries: ellagic acid; gut microbiome + anti-inflammatory support
- Strawberries: vitamin C; Lp(a) oxidation protection (LPA)

Frozen is equally effective. Year-round access. Add to fasted morning water (if tolerated during fast) or first meal.

### Eggs — MTHFR + CTH + FAM9B + Gut
**Eat: daily (2–4 eggs)**

Eggs are the most complete methylation food:
- Choline (found in yolk) is a methyl donor; compensates for MTHFR impairment
- Complete amino acid profile including cysteine (glutathione precursor for GSTP1)
- Vitamin B12 in yolk
- Lutein + zeaxanthin: brain and eye protection

Always eat the yolk. Pasture-raised preferred for omega-3 content.

### Liver (Beef/Chicken) — MTHFR + CTH + B-vitamins
**Eat: 1–2x/month (only if ferritin < 100 ng/mL)**

Liver is the most nutrient-dense food on Earth and the best natural source of:
- Folate (natural, not folic acid)
- B12 (methylcobalamin form)
- B6 (pyridoxal-5-phosphate form)
- Copper (balances zinc from FAM9B supplementation)
- CoQ10 precursors

**Note:** If your ferritin is above 100 ng/mL, limit liver to once monthly or less — it is also very high in iron.

### Olive Oil (Extra Virgin) — LPA + PON1 + Inflammation
**Use: as primary cooking/dressing oil**

EVOO contains oleocanthal (anti-inflammatory like ibuprofen), polyphenols (raise HDL, protect Lp(a) from oxidation), and oleic acid (anti-inflammatory). It actually *increases* PON1 activity — directly compensating for your PON1 rs854560 variant.

Use cold and for light sautéing (up to 375°F/190°C). Use avocado oil or ghee for higher heat.

---

### Brazil Nuts — TG Thyroid + GSTP1
**Eat: 1–2 nuts per day (not more)**

2 Brazil nuts contain ~150–200 mcg selenium — your optimal daily dose. This directly:
- Reduces thyroid antibodies (TG Hashimoto's variant)
- Boosts glutathione peroxidase function (GSTP1)
- Exceeding 3–4/day risks selenium toxicity over time

---

### Dark Chocolate (70%+) — CDKN2A/B + CLU + Mood
**Eat: 1–2 squares daily**

Cacao flavanols (epicatechin, procyanidins) are among the most studied cardiovascular nutrients. They:
- Improve arterial endothelial function (CDKN2 locus)
- Increase BDNF (brain health; CLU Alzheimer's)
- Increase serotonin precursor availability (HTR2A/SLC6A4)
- Reduce blood pressure modestly

Choose 70%+ cacao with no added milk.

## Meal Structure Template

### Breaking the Fast (12–1 PM)
- Protein-first: eggs, fish, or meat (4–6 oz)
- Vegetables: leafy greens, cruciferous, or roasted vegetables in olive oil/ghee
- Optional: small portion of berries as "dessert"
- Drink: water, herbal tea, or black coffee

### Dinner (6–8 PM)
- Protein: fatty fish 3–4x/week; grass-fed beef or lamb other nights; eggs always available
- Vegetables: large portion, varied; always include one cruciferous vegetable
- Healthy fat: olive oil drizzle, avocado, or handful of nuts (walnuts, almonds — not peanuts)
- Fermented food: small serving of kimchi, sauerkraut, or goat yogurt (if tolerated)
- Berries: if not eaten at lunch

### Smart Snacks (Within Eating Window Only)
- Walnuts + dark chocolate
- Sardines on cucumber rounds
- Eggs (hard-boiled)
- Guacamole + crudités
- Berries + goat yogurt
