## What This Means

CLU (clusterin) is a molecular chaperone and **autophagy regulator** — it helps clear misfolded proteins and cellular debris from the brain. The C/C variant (rs11136000) is one of the most replicated genetic associations with late-onset Alzheimer's disease in GWAS studies, second only to APOE4.

Specifically, this variant:
- Reduces the brain's clearance of amyloid-beta (the plaque that accumulates in Alzheimer's)
- Impairs autophagy (cellular self-cleaning)
- Increases neuroinflammation when systemic inflammation is present

**Critical context:** Like most of your risk variants, this is *inflammation-dependent*. A brain that isn't inflamed clears amyloid effectively even with this variant. The systemic inflammation from your gut (CDKAL1), cardiovascular (LPA, CTH), and dairy (MCM6) variants is the fuel that would actualize this risk.

## Protocol

**Intermittent Fasting — The Primary Intervention**
Autophagy (the cellular cleanup that CLU impairs when defective) is most powerfully triggered by fasting:
- **16:8 fasting minimum** (eat within an 8-hour window; skip breakfast)
- Your ADIPOQ variant (+/+) means you respond particularly well to IF — this is a case where two genes align perfectly
- Extended fasts (24–48 hours quarterly) provide deeper autophagy stimulation

**Physical Exercise (second most powerful):**
- Aerobic exercise increases BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) and upregulates autophagy in the brain
- Your ACTN3 endurance profile is built for this
- Target: 150–180 min/week cardiovascular exercise

**Anti-inflammatory brain diet:**
- **Omega-3 DHA** — 1–2 g/day DHA specifically (fish oil or algae oil); DHA is the primary brain structural fat and reduces amyloid burden
- Berries daily — anthocyanins cross the blood-brain barrier and directly reduce neuroinflammation
- Extra virgin olive oil — oleocanthal inhibits amyloid aggregation in vitro
- Avoid processed foods, seed oils, excess sugar — hyperglycemia is a major Alzheimer's risk ("Type 3 diabetes" hypothesis)

**Sleep:**
- The glymphatic system (brain's waste clearance) operates **during deep sleep**
- CLU impairs a daytime clearance mechanism; deep sleep is therefore even more critical for you
- Your ABCC9 sleep variant means you may need active sleep optimization (see [Sleep variant](../concerning/) and [Daily Rules](../../protocols/daily-rules.md))

**Cognitive engagement:**
- Learning new skills, complex cognitive tasks, and social engagement all reduce amyloid deposition independent of genetics
