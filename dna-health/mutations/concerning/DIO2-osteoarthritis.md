# 🟡 DIO2 — Osteoarthritis & Thyroid-Joint Axis

| Gene | DIO2 (Iodothyronine Deiodinase 2) |
|------|----------------------------------|
| Variants | rs12885300 (+/+) · rs225014 (+/-) |
| Classification | Double hit |
| Chromosome | 14 |
| Interaction | Compounds TG thyroid risk + HLA joint risk |

## What This Means

DIO2 converts the inactive thyroid hormone T4 into the active T3 within tissues (especially cartilage and joints). The DIO2 variant:
- **Reduces T4→T3 conversion in joint tissues** — cartilage is effectively thyroid-deficient even if your blood thyroid levels look normal
- Directly linked to **osteoarthritis** in multiple GWAS studies, particularly of the knee and hip
- Compounded by your multiple HLA rheumatoid arthritis variants

The rs12885300 +/+ alone confers a meaningful increase in osteoarthritis risk that is independent of body weight or activity level.

## Protocol

**Labs:**
- Full thyroid panel including **Free T3** and **Reverse T3** — your standard TSH and T4 might look normal while joint tissue is T3-deficient
- Target: Free T3 in the upper quartile of normal range

**Dietary:**
- Iodine 2x/week via seafood — iodine is the direct substrate for thyroid hormone
- Selenium 100–200 mcg/day — required for DIO2 enzyme function; also helps TG thyroid variant
- Collagen peptides 10–15 g/day — supports cartilage matrix repair

**Exercise:**
- Low-impact, high-rep training fits your ACTN3 slow-twitch profile and protects joints
- Swimming, cycling, rowing over high-impact running
- Strength training (deadlifts specifically per Wnt16 bone variant) — progressive load improves cartilage health via mechanostimulation

**If Symptomatic:**
- Ask specifically for T3 (liothyronine) or desiccated thyroid (Armour/NP Thyroid) — synthetic T4 alone may not address joint tissue T3 deficiency given this variant
