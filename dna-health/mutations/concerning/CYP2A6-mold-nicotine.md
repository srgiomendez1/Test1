# 🟡 CYP2A6 — Slow Mold & Nicotine Metabolism

| Gene | CYP2A6 (Cytochrome P450 Family 2 Subfamily A Member 6) |
|------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| rsID | rs1801272 |
| Classification | +/+ |
| Chromosome | 19 |

## What This Means

CYP2A6 is a liver enzyme responsible for metabolizing **nicotine** and certain **mycotoxins** (mold toxins). The +/+ variant significantly slows this metabolism:

**Nicotine:** Slower clearance means nicotine stays in your body longer. While this might sound like it reduces cravings between cigarettes, it actually means each exposure causes more prolonged harm and a stronger dependence signal per exposure.

**Mold toxins (mycotoxins):** This is the more overlooked and critical implication. Mold toxins from contaminated food or water-damaged buildings are cleared much more slowly. Combined with your impaired GSTP1 glutathione pathway, mold exposure can cause significant neurological, hormonal, and immune disruption in you that average people would shrug off.

## Protocol

**Mold avoidance:**
- **Inspect your home** for water damage, especially bathrooms, basements, AC units, behind appliances. Consider an air quality test.
- Store food properly — nuts (especially peanuts, pistachios), coffee, grains, dried fruit are common mycotoxin sources
- **Coffee:** Use high-quality, tested brands. "Bulletproof" and similar mold-tested coffees exist. Avoid cheap bulk coffee.
- Refrigerate bread and nuts; don't eat moldy food (obvious, but mycotoxins penetrate even "visually clean" areas of moldy food)

**If mold exposure is suspected:**
- GI Detox+ (pyropheophytin / activated charcoal / bentonite clay) — binds mycotoxins in the gut
- Cholestyramine (prescription) — most effective binder; discuss with physician
- Glutathione IV at a functional medicine clinic — accelerates excretion

**Nicotine:**
- Avoid all nicotine products — your slow metabolism means each puff delivers compounded harm
- If attempting cessation, note that your slower clearance may mean nicotine patches/gum work *better* for you (more stable blood levels), but also that cravings may last longer after quitting
