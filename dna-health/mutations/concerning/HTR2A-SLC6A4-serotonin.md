## What This Means

You have homozygous risk variants in **both** the serotonin receptor and the serotonin transporter:

**HTR2A (rs6313):** Affects how efficiently serotonin signals are received at receptor sites. The risk variant is associated with:
- Higher baseline anxiety and depression susceptibility
- Altered pain perception and mood regulation
- Also directly affects joint tissue — HTR2A receptors are present in cartilage and blood vessels

**SLC6A4 (rs140701):** The serotonin transporter gene, similar to what SSRIs target pharmacologically. Risk variant affects serotonin recycling at synapses, potentially reducing serotonin availability.

**Key fact:** 90% of serotonin is made in the gut (enterochromaffin cells). Your gut variants (CDKAL1 leaky gut) directly undermine serotonin production. The brain and gut serotonin issues are inseparable.

## Protocol

**The gut-brain serotonin axis — fix the gut first:**
- Heal leaky gut (see [CDKAL1 file](CDKAL1-PON1-gut.md)) — this is the primary lever
- Eat fermented foods: kimchi, sauerkraut, goat kefir (if tolerated), kombucha
- Prebiotic fiber: chicory, garlic, onions, leeks, asparagus — feed serotonin-producing gut bacteria
- Avoid antibiotics unless medically necessary (discuss alternatives with your doctor)

**Serotonin precursors:**
- **5-HTP** — 50–100 mg at night; direct serotonin precursor (from L-tryptophan). Take with B6 (P5P) for conversion. *Note: Do not combine with SSRIs or MAOIs without physician guidance.*
- **L-Tryptophan** — 500–1,000 mg at night; gentler alternative to 5-HTP
- **Magnesium glycinate** — 200–400 mg at night; required co-factor for serotonin synthesis

**Lifestyle (most potent interventions for serotonin):**
- **Sunlight in the morning** (10–20 min outdoor light exposure) — triggers serotonin synthesis; also helps your circadian rhythm
- **Exercise** — cardio in particular (running, cycling) is the most studied natural serotonin booster; matches your ACTN3 endurance profile
- **Cold exposure** — cold showers or cold plunges transiently increase serotonin and dopamine
- **Social connection** — prosocial behavior directly stimulates serotonin release

**If experiencing depression or anxiety:**
- Consult a psychiatrist about SSRI/SNRI compatibility with your specific genotype; your SLC6A4 variant is one that is specifically studied in pharmacogenomics
- *Avoid starting SSRIs during acute gut inflammation — address gut health concurrently*
