# 🟡 MTHFD1 — Secondary Folate Pathway Impairment

| Gene | MTHFD1 (Methylenetetrahydrofolate Dehydrogenase 1) |
|------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| rsID | rs2236225 |
| Your Genotype | Homozygous risk (confirmed via PDF report) |
| Classification | +/+ |
| Chromosome | 14 |

## What This Means

MTHFD1 works **upstream** of MTHFR in the folate metabolism pathway. It converts folate derivatives into the form that MTHFR then processes into 5-MTHF. Having MTHFD1 +/+ on top of MTHFR C677T +/+ means **both steps of folate processing are impaired**.

Think of it as: even before the folate reaches the broken MTHFR enzyme, it's already being processed sub-optimally by MTHFD1.

**Effect:** Your need for pre-formed, active methylfolate (5-MTHF) is even higher than MTHFR alone would suggest.

## Protocol

- Your 5-MTHF dose should be on the **higher end**: 800–1,000 mcg/day
- Ensure dietary folate (not folic acid) from leafy greens, avocado, berries
- Avoid folic acid in all forms (also compounds MTHFD1 impairment)
- This variant is particularly important in pregnancy planning — discuss with your doctor
