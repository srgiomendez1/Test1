# 🟡 MCM6 — Lactose Intolerance (Lactase Non-Persistence)

| Gene | MCM6 (Minichromosome Maintenance Complex 6) |
|------|---------------------------------------------|
| Variants | rs4988235 (+/+) · rs182549 (+/+) |
| Your Genotype | rs4988235: G/G · rs182549: C/C (confirmed AncestryDNA) |
| Effect | Homozygous lactase non-persistence |
| Chromosome | 2 |

> **Accuracy Note (Important):** The original report stated that MCM6 +/+ individuals have inflammatory responses to cow dairy "casein A1" protein. This is **incorrect as stated**. The MCM6 gene variants *only* affect **lactase enzyme activity** — they control whether your body continues producing lactase into adulthood. They have no direct relationship to casein protein sensitivity. A1 beta-casein (a separate cow milk protein) can cause GI symptoms in some people through a different, unrelated mechanism (release of beta-casomorphin-7 from A1 digestion), but this is NOT what these gene variants detect. The recommendation to avoid cow dairy remains reasonable if you are lactose intolerant, but the mechanism in the original report was conflated. This has been corrected.

## What This Means

MCM6 contains regulatory elements that control the **LCT gene** (lactase enzyme). The G/G (rs4988235) and C/C (rs182549) genotypes are the ancestral, non-persistent forms — meaning your body **does not produce adequate lactase in adulthood**, making you unable to efficiently digest lactose (milk sugar).

**What is true:**
- You are genetically lactose intolerant
- Undigested lactose in the colon causes gas, bloating, diarrhea, and GI discomfort
- This affects gut comfort and may feed dysbiosis (connects to your CDKAL1 IBD risk indirectly)

**What is NOT supported by this gene:**
- Casein A1 inflammatory responses are a separate phenomenon not linked to MCM6/LCT variants
- Whey protein inflammation is a separate mechanism unrelated to this gene

**Practical outcome:** The recommendation to avoid cow dairy is still appropriate — if you're lactose intolerant (confirmed by genetics here), dairy will cause GI distress and dysbiosis. Goat and sheep dairy contain less A1 casein and are lower in lactose, making them better tolerated by many people with this genotype.

## Allowed / Not Allowed

| ❌ Avoid Completely | ✅ May Be Fine |
|--------------------|---------------|
| Cow milk | Ghee (pure fat, no casein/lactose) |
| Cow cheese | Goat milk/cheese/yogurt* |
| Whey protein (cow) | Sheep milk/cheese* |
| Cow yogurt/kefir | Goat whey protein |
| Ice cream | Coconut/almond/oat alternatives |
| Butter (standard) | Grass-fed butter (small amounts)* |

*Test goat/sheep dairy by drinking a large glass on an empty stomach. If no GI reaction within 4 hours, you may tolerate it.

## Protocol

- **Read all labels** for "whey," "casein," "milk solids," "lactose" — these appear in protein bars, processed foods, restaurant sauces
- Protein alternatives: eggs, fish, goat whey, pea protein, hemp protein
- Calcium sources without dairy: sardines (with bones), almonds, kale, broccoli, fortified non-dairy milk
