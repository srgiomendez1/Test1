# 🟡 SOD2 — Iron Dysregulation & Oxidative Stress

| Gene | SOD2 (Superoxide Dismutase 2) |
|------|-------------------------------|
| rsID | rs4880 |
| Your Genotype | A/A (= Val/Val homozygous) |
| Classification | +/+ Homozygous |
| Chromosome | 6 |

## What This Means

SOD2 is your **mitochondrial antioxidant defense**. The Val variant (A on + strand) reduces how efficiently this enzyme is imported into mitochondria, leading to higher mitochondrial oxidative stress. This variant also affects iron metabolism:
- Excess free iron acts as a catalyst for oxidative damage (Fenton reaction)
- Val/Val individuals tend to accumulate iron more harmfully in tissues

## Protocol

**Blood tests:**
- Ferritin: Target **50–100 ng/mL**. If > 150: donate blood, drink black tea with meals (reduces iron absorption by 60–70%)
- If < 50: ensure adequate red meat and liver intake

**Antioxidant support:**
- CoQ10 (ubiquinol form) — 100–200 mg/day: directly scavenges mitochondrial superoxide
- Vitamin C — 500–1,000 mg/day: regenerates other antioxidants
- Magnesium — 200–400 mg at night: co-factor for SOD2 function

**Avoid:**
- Iron supplements unless medically indicated (your ferritin may run high)
- Excessive red meat if ferritin is already > 100 ng/mL
- Alcohol — dramatically increases iron absorption and oxidative stress
