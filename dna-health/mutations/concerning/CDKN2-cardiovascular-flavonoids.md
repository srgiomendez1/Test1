## What This Means

The CDKN2A/2B region (chromosome 9p21) is the **single most replicated cardiovascular GWAS locus** — it has appeared in essentially every large-scale heart disease genetic study since 2007. Having all three variants homozygous (+/+) indicates meaningfully elevated risk for:
- Coronary artery disease (CAD)
- Myocardial infarction (heart attack)
- Peripheral artery disease
- This region also influences cell senescence — aging of blood vessel cells

The specific intervention flagged by your genetic analysis is **flavonoids** — a class of plant compounds with direct evidence for protecting the cardiovascular system, particularly at this locus.

## Protocol

**Eat berries every single day (non-negotiable if blood sugar is controlled):**
- Blueberries, blackberries, raspberries, strawberries — highest in anthocyanins
- Target: 1/2 to 1 cup daily
- Frozen is acceptable and equally effective

**Other high-flavonoid foods:**
- Dark chocolate (70%+ cacao, 1–2 squares/day)
- Green tea / matcha (2–3 cups/day)
- Red/purple grapes
- Pomegranate
- Quercetin-rich foods: red onion, capers, apples with skin

**Flavonoid supplements (if diet is inconsistent):**
- **Quercetin** — 500–1,000 mg/day; one of the most studied cardiovascular flavonoids
- **Resveratrol** — 100–500 mg/day (trans-resveratrol form); activates SIRT1/AMPK pathways that offset 9p21 risk
- **Pterostilbene** — more bioavailable analog of resveratrol; 50–100 mg/day
- **Grape seed extract (OPC)** — 100–300 mg/day; oligomeric proanthocyanidins, specifically studied for arterial health

**Note on blood sugar:** All these benefits require blood sugar to be controlled. Berries + hyperglycemia = worsened inflammation. Keep fasted glucose < 85 mg/dL.
