# 🟡 TG Gene — Autoimmune Thyroid Disease Risk

**Status: HETEROZYGOUS — Monitoring Recommended (Risk Magnitude Uncertain)**

> **Accuracy Note:** The original AJ Consulting report claimed a "10-fold increased risk" for rs180223 and "2-fold" for rs2076740. A systematic meta-analysis of 10 case-control studies (3,013 cases, 1,812 controls) published in *Genes & Immunity* (2018) found **no significant independent association** between rs180223 and autoimmune thyroid disease (AITD). rs2076740 showed a weak signal in one model, but this was sensitive to study selection and ethnic composition. The absolute risk from these specific SNPs appears more modest and uncertain than the original report stated. Thyroid monitoring remains prudent given the overall gene region, but the dramatic risk numbers are not supported by best available evidence.

## What This Gene Does

The TG gene encodes **thyroglobulin**, the backbone protein onto which iodine attaches to make thyroid hormones (T3 and T4). Variants in this gene may alter the protein's shape, potentially influencing how the immune system recognizes it. This is the proposed mechanism by which TG SNPs could contribute to Hashimoto's thyroiditis (autoimmune hypothyroidism).

## What the Evidence Actually Shows

**What is well-established:**
- The TG gene region is genuinely implicated in autoimmune thyroid disease in GWAS studies
- Thyroglobulin antibodies (TGAb) are a direct marker of Hashimoto's attacks on this protein
- Your combined TG gene variants (rs180223 + rs2076740) are worth monitoring

**What is NOT well-established:**
- The "10x risk" for rs180223 cited by the original report is not replicated in the best meta-analysis data
- These specific SNPs, in isolation, show inconsistent associations across populations
- Risk appears population-specific (stronger in some Asian populations, weaker in Caucasians)

**Practical bottom line:** You have heterozygous variants in the TG gene region. Whether this meaningfully elevates your individual thyroid autoimmunity risk beyond the general population baseline is genuinely uncertain. The monitoring recommendations below remain sensible regardless — thyroid issues are common, and early detection is easy.

## What Hashimoto's Does If Untreated

- Chronic fatigue and brain fog
- Weight gain, cold intolerance
- Depression and anxiety (connects to your HTR2A serotonin variants)
- Hair thinning, dry skin
- Joint pain (connects to your DIO2 + HLA joint variants)
- Eventual hypothyroidism requiring lifelong hormone replacement

## Actionable Protocol

### Blood Tests (Recommended, Not Urgent)
Order these at your next annual physical and repeat annually:
- **TSH** — Target: 0.5–1.5 mIU/L
- **TPO antibodies (anti-TPO)** — Should be negative
- **TGAb (anti-thyroglobulin antibodies)** — Should be negative; specifically relevant to TG gene
- **Free T4 and Free T3**
- **Reverse T3** — Elevated rT3 indicates thyroid conversion problems (also relevant to DIO2 variant)

If antibodies are elevated, work with a physician to address gut inflammation, optimize vitamin D and selenium, and reassess.

### Dietary Interventions
- **Selenium** 100–200 mcg/day (selenomethionine) — the most evidence-backed intervention for reducing thyroid antibodies; multiple meta-analyses confirm benefit for Hashimoto's patients
- **Iodine via seafood** 2x/week — iodine is required for thyroid hormone; deficiency is a known Hashimoto's trigger
- **Anti-inflammatory diet** — relevant given the inflammation-dependent nature of autoimmune thyroid disease
- **Optimize gut health** — leaky gut and dysbiosis are associated with Hashimoto's via molecular mimicry (connected to your IBD risk from CDKAL1)

### Supplements
- **Selenium** — 150–200 mcg/day selenomethionine
- **Vitamin D3** — 2,000–5,000 IU/day (you need this for CYP2R1 variant regardless)
- **Magnesium glycinate** — 200–400 mg at night; supports thyroid hormone conversion

### If Medical Treatment Is Needed
- Request **Armour Thyroid or NP Thyroid** (desiccated thyroid) rather than synthetic T4 only — relevant if your DIO2 variant is impairing T4→T3 conversion
