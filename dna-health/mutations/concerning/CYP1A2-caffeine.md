## What This Means

CYP1A2 is the primary enzyme that breaks down caffeine in the liver. The A/C heterozygous variant means you are an **intermediate metabolizer** — caffeine stays in your system **longer than average but not as long as a slow metabolizer**.

Practical effects:
- An afternoon cup of coffee (e.g., 2–3 PM) may still affect your sleep quality at 11 PM
- Caffeine's anxiety-amplifying effect (via ADORA2A variant) lasts longer per dose
- Adrenaline from caffeine (via adrenal axis) stays elevated longer

## Protocol

- **Caffeine cutoff: 12–1 PM** for most people; for you, even earlier may be warranted if sleep is disrupted
- **Limit to 1–2 cups of coffee in the morning** (150–200 mg caffeine total)
- **Green tea instead of coffee in the afternoon** — lower caffeine with L-theanine (blunts anxiety response)
- Monitor your response — track how late-day caffeine affects your sleep using subjective quality or an Oura Ring
- If anxious or wired: your ADORA2A + SLC6A4 + CYP1A2 triple combination means caffeine is a net negative; consider switching to decaf or matcha (very low caffeine with high L-theanine)
