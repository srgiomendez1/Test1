## What This Means

FAM9B is an X-linked gene involved in testosterone regulation. The T allele (rs5934505) is associated with **increased susceptibility to suboptimal testosterone levels**. Low testosterone compounds multiple other variants in your profile:
- **Joints:** IL2RA (joint inflammation) and HLA RA variants are testosterone-sensitive — testosterone is anti-inflammatory
- **Bones:** Wnt16 bone density variant requires adequate testosterone to maintain density
- **Mood:** Serotonin pathways (HTR2A) are modulated by testosterone
- **Muscle:** Your ACTN3 slow-twitch profile still requires adequate testosterone for mass maintenance

## Target Levels
- **Total Testosterone:** 500–1,500 ng/dL (17–50 nmol/L)
- **Free Testosterone:** 15–25 pg/mL
- **SHBG:** Should not be excessively high (SHBG binds testosterone, reducing free/active fraction)
- **LH and FSH:** To understand whether any deficit is primary (testicular) or secondary (pituitary)

## Protocol

**Test first:** Get a comprehensive male hormone panel at 8–10 AM (testosterone is highest in the morning).

**Lifestyle to optimize testosterone naturally:**
- **Zinc + Copper** — Zinc 25–30 mg + Copper 1–2 mg/day. Zinc deficiency is the single most common nutritional cause of low testosterone. Take with food.
- **Vitamin D3** — 2,000–5,000 IU/day; directly increases testosterone production (Leydig cell function). You need this anyway for CYP2R1.
- **Sleep** — testosterone is synthesized during deep sleep. 7–9 hours is non-negotiable. (See ABCC9 sleep variant.)
- **Resistance training** — compound lifts (squat, deadlift, bench) with progressive overload. Your ACTN3 profile favors volume; 4–5 sets of 8–15 reps.
- **Body fat** — keep below 20% body fat; excess adipose tissue converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase
- **Stress management** — cortisol directly suppresses testosterone production
- **Minimize alcohol** — even moderate alcohol reduces testosterone 24–48 hours later

**Avoid:**
- **Plastic containers / BPA** — environmental estrogen; your ESR1 variant makes you more estrogen-sensitive
- **Seed oils** — high omega-6 impairs Leydig cell testosterone synthesis
- **Xenoestrogens** — parabens in personal care products, pesticide residues on non-organic produce

**If levels are persistently low (< 400 ng/dL with symptoms):**
- Consult a urologist or men's health specialist about TRT (Testosterone Replacement Therapy) or Clomid (clomiphene citrate — stimulates natural production)
