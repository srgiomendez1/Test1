## What This Means

IL2RA encodes the alpha subunit of the interleukin-2 receptor, a key regulator of immune cell activation. The T/T variant is associated with heightened immune responses in joint tissue, increasing susceptibility to inflammatory joint disease. This variant operates synergistically with your HLA-DRB1/DRB9/DQA1 RA risk variants.

The critical trigger is **testosterone.** IL2RA-driven joint inflammation is partially regulated by testosterone — testosterone is anti-inflammatory and suppresses excessive IL-2 receptor signaling.

## Protocol

- **Optimize testosterone** (see [FAM9B file](FAM9B-testosterone.md)) — this is the master lever for this variant
- **Anti-inflammatory diet** (eliminates triggers; see [Foods to Avoid](../../protocols/foods-avoid.md))
- **Omega-3 EPA+DHA** — 2–4 g/day; directly inhibits the IL-2 pathway and downstream inflammatory signaling
- **Movement throughout the day** — avoid sitting > 45 minutes without movement; joint inflammation worsens with stasis
- **Maintain blood sugar < 85 mg/dL** — hyperglycemia amplifies IL-2 receptor-driven inflammation
