## What This Means — Mixed Signal

SCN9A encodes Nav1.7, a sodium channel critical for pain signal transmission in peripheral nerves. The +/+ variant is associated with **reduced pain sensitivity** — you feel less pain than the average person.

**The advantage:**
- Higher pain threshold in training — you can push through discomfort others would stop at
- Less suffering from minor injuries, dental procedures, etc.

**The critical caution:**
- **Pain is a protection signal.** Reduced pain sensitivity means injuries can progress further before you notice them
- Joint damage, stress fractures, and internal problems may not trigger the pain response that would normally prompt you to stop or seek care
- This is especially relevant given your HLA rheumatoid arthritis risk and Wnt16 bone density variant — you could be developing joint or bone problems without adequate warning signals

## Protocol

- **Don't train through discomfort blindly** — distinguish between productive training discomfort (muscle burn, fatigue) and structural warning signals (sharp pain, joint pain, localized pain)
- **Get regular imaging** if you have any musculoskeletal concerns — don't rely on pain as your only guide
- **Monitor joint health actively** (see [Lab Tests Guide](../../protocols/lab-tests.md))
- **Post-workout body scan** — do a deliberate 2-minute scan of joints and muscles after workouts to notice subtle signals you might otherwise ignore
