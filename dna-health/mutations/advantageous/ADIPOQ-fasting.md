## What This Means — This Is Good

Adiponectin is an anti-inflammatory hormone produced by fat tissue that regulates insulin sensitivity, fat burning, and metabolic health. The ADIPOQ +/+ variant means your **adiponectin response to fasting is enhanced** — when you skip breakfast or extend your fasting window, your body releases more adiponectin than average.

Benefits for you:
- **Better fat burning** during the fasted state
- **Better insulin sensitivity** through fasting
- **More anti-inflammatory** benefit from fasting than the average person
- Directly counteracts several of your risk variants (CLU Alzheimer's, CDKN2 cardiovascular, HLA joint risks — all inflammation-mediated)

## How to Leverage This

**Intermittent Fasting Protocol (tailored to your genetics):**
- **Skip breakfast** — this is explicitly recommended for your ADIPOQ variant
- **16:8 minimum:** Eat between 12 PM–8 PM (or 1 PM–9 PM)
- **18:6 optimal:** Eat between 1 PM–7 PM for maximum adiponectin release
- **Weekly 24-hour fast:** Once per week (dinner to dinner); significantly boosts autophagy (also benefits your CLU Alzheimer's risk)

**What to do during the fast:**
- Black coffee (morning only — caffeine cutoff at 1 PM per your CYP1A2 variant)
- Water, plain sparkling water, electrolytes if needed
- Green tea (low caffeine)

**Breaking the fast:**
- First meal should be protein-first (eggs, fish, meat) to blunt blood sugar spike
- Avoid starting with fruit, bread, or high-carb foods
- Berries are the ideal "dessert" to break a fast (CDKAL1, CDKN2 benefit)

**Note on metformin:** Your ATM variant (+/+) makes you a poor responder to metformin. Berberine (500–1,500 mg/day with meals) is a natural alternative with similar mechanisms and no metformin drawbacks.
