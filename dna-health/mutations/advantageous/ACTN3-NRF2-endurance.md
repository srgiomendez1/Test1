## What This Means — This Is Good

**ACTN3 (rs1815739 T/T — the XX genotype):**
Alpha-actinin-3 is a structural protein found *only* in fast-twitch muscle fibers. The T/T genotype produces a stop codon — no functional alpha-actinin-3 is made. This sounds like a deficit, but it's actually an **evolutionary advantage for endurance**:
- Greater efficiency in oxidative (aerobic) energy metabolism
- Enhanced muscle glycogen storage
- Better endurance performance and recovery
- Higher mitochondrial density in muscles
- Your muscles are wired for volume, endurance, and sustained effort — not one-rep max power

**NRF2 (rs7181866 +/-):**
NRF2 is the master regulator of the body's antioxidant response. The +/- endurance variant confers a physiological adaptation advantage during sustained aerobic exercise — your antioxidant upregulation in response to exercise is enhanced.

## How to Leverage This

**Training:**
- Higher rep ranges (8–20 reps) build more muscle for you than low-rep powerlifting
- Circuit training, AMRAP, supersets — your muscle fibers recover faster between sets
- Endurance sports: running, cycling, rowing, swimming, triathlon — you are genetically competitive here
- 45–90 minute training sessions with moderate weights, high volume
- Cardio: 3–5 sessions/week; Zone 2 heart rate (conversation pace) for longevity

**Recovery:**
- Your endurance fibers recover faster — you can train 5–6 days/week if nutrition and sleep are adequate
- Active recovery (light walking, yoga, swimming) on "rest" days benefits your fiber type

**Body composition:**
- Combined with ADIPOQ (intermittent fasting gene), you are efficient at fat-burning during fasted states
- Lean muscle mass comes easily with consistent volume training
