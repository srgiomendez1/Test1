## What This Gene Does

The LPA gene encodes apolipoprotein(a), a protein component of Lipoprotein(a) — often called Lp(a). Lp(a) is a modified form of LDL cholesterol. Your Lp(a) levels are **primarily determined by genetics (80–90%)**, not diet or exercise. The rs10455872 G allele is one of the strongest known genetic drivers of elevated Lp(a).

## What the LPA +/+ Means for You

Being homozygous G/G for rs10455872 is associated with **significantly elevated Lp(a)** — often > 2–5x the population average. Elevated Lp(a):
- Promotes atherosclerosis (arterial plaque formation)
- Is pro-thrombotic (increases clotting risk)
- Is pro-inflammatory to arterial walls
- Is **independent of standard LDL cholesterol** — you can have normal LDL and still have dangerous Lp(a)
- Is **not lowered by statins** (statins may actually raise Lp(a) slightly)

## Why This Is Dangerous

Lp(a) is the single strongest genetic risk factor for early-onset cardiovascular disease. Having G/G at rs10455872:
- Increases risk of **myocardial infarction (heart attack)** by ~2–3x
- Increases risk of **aortic valve calcification**
- Increases **stroke risk**
- The risk is compounded by your CTH (homocysteine) and CDKN2A/B variants

## Actionable Protocol

### Blood Monitoring (Critical)
- **Test Lp(a) levels immediately and every 6 months**
- Target: **< 65 nmol/L** (equivalent to ~30 mg/dL — note: two different units are used; confirm with your lab which unit they report)
- If your Lp(a) is > 125 nmol/L (> 50 mg/dL): consult a cardiologist who is familiar with Lp(a)

### Supplements That Help Lower Lp(a)
- **Nicotinamide (Niacinamide / Vitamin B3)** — the most evidence-backed natural Lp(a) reducer
  - Dose: 500 mg–1,500 mg/day (flush-free form or timed-release)
  - Studies show 20–30% reduction in Lp(a)
  - Start low and work up; monitor liver enzymes if using high doses
- **Vitamin C** — 1,000–2,000 mg/day; reduces oxidized Lp(a) and arterial inflammation
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** — 2–4 g EPA+DHA/day (fish oil, not krill oil for this purpose)

### Lifestyle Interventions
- **Sauna** (4–5x/week, 20+ minutes) — shown to reduce cardiovascular risk markers, including some Lp(a) reduction
- **Sun exposure** — natural vitamin D synthesis supports cardiovascular health
- **Blood donation** — reduces iron load and slightly reduces viscosity/clotting risk
- **Anti-inflammatory diet** — inflammation amplifies Lp(a)'s damage to arteries

### Avoid
- **Seed oils (vegetable oils)** — oxidized LDL compounds Lp(a) damage (you also have PON1 rs854560 +/+)
- **Trans fats** — severely pro-atherogenic
- **Smoking** — dramatically amplifies Lp(a) cardiovascular risk
- **Excess sugar** — increases inflammation that Lp(a) depends on to cause damage

### Emerging Treatments (Discuss with Cardiologist)
- **Inclisiran** (siRNA drug) and **Pelacarsen** (antisense oligonucleotide) are in advanced trials specifically targeting Lp(a) — these may become available soon
- **PCSK9 inhibitors** have modest Lp(a) lowering effects (~25–30%)
