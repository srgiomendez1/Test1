## What This Gene Does

CTH encodes cystathionine gamma-lyase, a key enzyme in the **transsulfuration pathway** — the process that converts homocysteine into cysteine and ultimately into glutathione (your master antioxidant). When CTH is impaired, homocysteine cannot be efficiently cleared via this pathway.

You have two separate, compounding hits to homocysteine clearance:
1. **MTHFR C677T +/+** — impairs the methylation pathway (homocysteine → methionine)
2. **CTH +/+** — impairs the transsulfuration pathway (homocysteine → cysteine)

This is the genetic equivalent of having both exits blocked. Homocysteine elevation is almost certain without active intervention.

## What High Homocysteine Does

Elevated homocysteine is a potent vascular and neurological toxin:
- **Damages arterial endothelium** (inner lining of blood vessels) — directly causes atherosclerosis
- **Increases thrombosis risk** (blood clot formation)
- **Neurotoxic** at high levels — associated with cognitive decline and depression
- **Elevates Lp(a) damage** — your LPA variant makes this even more critical
- Acts as a pro-inflammatory signal throughout the body

## Actionable Protocol

### Blood Monitoring
- **Test homocysteine immediately** — it's a cheap, standard blood test
- Target: **< 7 µmol/L** (optimal); < 10 µmol/L (acceptable); > 15 = action required
- Test every 6 months while managing; annually once stable

### Supplements That Directly Lower Homocysteine
- **5-MTHF (methylfolate)** — 400–800 mcg/day (already prescribed for MTHFR — covers both)
- **Methylcobalamin (B12)** — 500–1,000 mcg/day sublingual; B12 is a required co-factor
- **Pyridoxal-5-Phosphate (P5P)** — active form of B6, 25–50 mg/day; directly supports the transsulfuration pathway
- **Betaine (TMG)** — 500–1,500 mg/day; alternative methylation donor that can recycle homocysteine independently of MTHFR
- **Riboflavin (B2)** — 10–20 mg/day; co-factor for MTHFR enzyme activity

### Dietary Support
- **High-quality animal protein** — methionine cycle requires adequate substrate; meat, eggs, fish provide B12 and amino acids
- **Liver** (if ferritin < 100 ng/mL) — richest food source of all B-vitamins needed here
- **Beets / beet root** — contain betaine naturally
- **Leafy greens** — natural folate sources

### Avoid
- **Alcohol** — major homocysteine elevator; blocks B-vitamin absorption
- **Coffee in excess** — > 3 cups/day can raise homocysteine modestly
- **Folic acid** (synthetic) — use methylfolate instead
- **Nitrous oxide** — destroys B12 (avoid at dental procedures; discuss alternatives with dentist)
