## What This Gene Does

GSTP1 encodes glutathione S-transferase Pi 1, one of the body's primary **phase II detoxification enzymes**. It conjugates (binds) glutathione to electrophilic and oxidized compounds, marking them for excretion. It is highly expressed in liver, lungs, and brain.

The rs1695 G/G (Ile105Val, Val/Val) variant changes the enzyme's three-dimensional structure, reducing its catalytic efficiency for many substrates compared to the Ile/Ile genotype.

## What the GSTP1 Val/Val Means for You

**Clearly supported:**
- **Reduced clearance of oxidized lipids** — oxidized polyunsaturated fats (from seed oils, fried foods) are conjugated by GSTP1; Val/Val clears these more slowly → they accumulate and damage cell membranes and arterial walls. This pairs directly with your PON1 +/+ variant.
- **Reduced clearance of reactive oxygen species (ROS)** — Val/Val has lower superoxide and peroxide neutralization capacity via the glutathione system
- **Impaired detox of some environmental carcinogens** — reduced clearance of tobacco smoke metabolites, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, and some industrial chemicals

**More nuanced / uncertain:**
- **Heavy metals:** The Val105 variant alters enzyme kinetics for metal-glutathione conjugates, but clinical studies show mixed results. A study in children found Val/Val *may* handle mercury differently (and potentially better) than expected. The blanket "struggle to eliminate heavy metals" claim from the original report is not clearly supported. Minimize metal exposure as a precaution, but don't treat this as a confirmed heavy metal accumulation gene.

**Net practical implication:** Your greatest GSTP1-related risk is from **oxidized fats and environmental chemical exposures**, not necessarily heavy metal accumulation specifically.

## Actionable Protocol

### Minimize Oxidized Fat and Chemical Exposure (Highest Priority)
- **Zero seed oils** — canola, soybean, sunflower, corn, cottonseed; oxidized PUFAs are your clearest GSTP1 risk (also see PON1 file)
- **No smoking / secondhand smoke** — polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons are a primary GSTP1 substrate; Val/Val clears these more slowly
- **Minimize chemical fragrance / product exposure** — parabens, phthalates; Val/Val reduces clearance of these xenoestrogens (also relevant to your ESR1 estrogen sensitivity)
- **Cook at lower temperatures** — avoid charring/burning meat; reduces carcinogen load that GSTP1 normally clears

### Glutathione Support (Compensates for Reduced GSTP1)
- **N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)** — 600–1,200 mg/day; best glutathione precursor; also broadly supports detox capacity
- **Liposomal Glutathione** — 250–500 mg/day; direct supplementation
- **Alpha-Lipoic Acid (ALA)** — 300–600 mg/day; regenerates glutathione and is a broad-spectrum antioxidant
- **Selenium** — 150–200 mcg/day; required for glutathione peroxidase enzymes (also benefits thyroid)

### Dietary Glutathione Boosters
- **Cruciferous vegetables daily** — broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cauliflower, kale; sulforaphane dramatically upregulates the Nrf2 pathway, increasing glutathione synthesis and compensating partially for reduced GSTP1
- **Sulfur-rich foods** — garlic, onions, eggs: provide cysteine for glutathione
- **Turmeric/curcumin** — 500 mg standardized extract; upregulates Nrf2 → glutathione

### Practical Metal Exposure Precautions (Reasonable, Not Urgent)
- Limit high-mercury fish (swordfish, king mackerel, albacore tuna in excess)
- Filter tap water if in an older building (lead pipes pre-1986)
- If concerned: a hair heavy metals test can establish a baseline — this is reasonable for anyone with GSTP1 variants, though not urgent
