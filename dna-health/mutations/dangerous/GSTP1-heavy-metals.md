## What This Gene Does

GSTP1 encodes glutathione S-transferase Pi 1, one of the body's primary **phase II detoxification enzymes**. It conjugates (binds) glutathione to toxic compounds — including heavy metals, carcinogens, and oxidative stress byproducts — marking them for excretion. It is expressed in high concentration in the liver, lungs, and brain.

The rs1695 G/G (Ile105Val homozygous) variant reduces GSTP1 enzyme activity significantly, particularly its ability to neutralize heavy metals and carcinogenic compounds.

## What the GSTP1 +/+ Means for You

- Your body accumulates heavy metals **faster than average** and clears them **more slowly**
- Heavy metals you are particularly poor at clearing: **mercury, lead, arsenic, cadmium**
- This pairs dangerously with your **poor diet of seed oils** (PON1 +/+) — oxidative stress generated by metals overwhelms your already-reduced detox capacity
- GSTP1 also protects against certain cancer-causing chemicals — reduced activity means higher susceptibility to environmental carcinogens
- Your supporting GSTP1 variants (+/- on rs1871042, rs749174, rs947895) indicate the impairment is systemic across the GSTP1 family

## Actionable Protocol

### Testing
- **Get a hair heavy metals test** — hair testing captures months of accumulation and is more reliable than blood for chronic exposure
  - Focus on: mercury (Hg), lead (Pb), arsenic (As), cadmium (Cd), aluminum (Al)
- If any metals are elevated: repeat test in 6 months after interventions

### Minimize Exposure (Most Important)
- **Mercury**: Limit large, long-lived fish — swordfish, king mackerel, tuna (especially albacore). Sardines and salmon are safe. Avoid amalgam (silver) dental fillings; if you have them, discuss safe removal with a biological dentist.
- **Lead**: Old pipes in the home (pre-1986) — filter tap water. Avoid renovation dust in older homes without proper protection.
- **Arsenic**: Some rice products and well water. Vary grains; filter well water.
- **Cadmium**: Smoking is the #1 source. Avoid secondhand smoke.
- **Aluminum**: Avoid aluminum cookware, deodorants with aluminum, and excessive antacid use.
- **Mold toxins**: Your CYP2A6 variant also impairs mold detox — mold exposure is an independent threat. Inspect your home.

### Glutathione Support (Critical)
Since GSTP1 requires glutathione as a substrate, keeping glutathione levels high partially compensates:
- **N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)** — 600–1,200 mg/day; best precursor to glutathione, also chelates some heavy metals directly
- **Liposomal Glutathione** — 250–500 mg/day; direct supplementation
- **Alpha-Lipoic Acid (ALA)** — 300–600 mg/day; regenerates glutathione and is a chelating agent (take with meals to avoid blood sugar drops)
- **Selenium** — 100–200 mcg/day; required for glutathione peroxidase enzymes (also benefits your thyroid TG variant)

### Dietary Glutathione Boosters
- **Cruciferous vegetables** — broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cauliflower, kale: upregulate glutathione synthesis
- **Sulfur-rich foods** — garlic, onions, eggs: provide cysteine for glutathione
- **Whey protein** — high in cysteine (note: use goat whey due to MCM6 cow dairy variant)
- **Turmeric/curcumin** — upregulates Nrf2 pathway, increasing glutathione synthesis

### If Heavy Metals Are Confirmed High
- Seek **IV glutathione infusions** at a functional medicine clinic — most effective acute intervention
- Consider physician-supervised chelation if levels are significantly elevated
- Re-test after 3 months of intervention
