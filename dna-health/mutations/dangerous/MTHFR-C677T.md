## What This Gene Does

MTHFR converts folate (vitamin B9) into 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (5-MTHF), the active form the body uses for:
- **Methylation** — turning genes on/off, detoxification, neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Homocysteine clearance** — converting toxic homocysteine back to methionine
- **DNA synthesis and repair**
- **Serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine production**

## What the C677T +/+ Means for You

The T/T (homozygous) variant reduces MTHFR enzyme activity by **60–70%** compared to normal. This means:
- Your body cannot efficiently convert folic acid → active folate
- Homocysteine builds up (cardiovascular and neurological toxin)
- Methylation across the body is impaired
- Risk of depression, anxiety, fatigue is elevated
- DNA repair is slower

## Why This Is Dangerous

Homozygous MTHFR C677T is associated with:
- **Elevated cardiovascular disease risk** (via homocysteine elevation)
- **Neural tube defects** in offspring
- **Increased stroke risk** (particularly at young ages)
- **Treatment-resistant depression** if not managed
- Compounds your LPA and CTH variants (see those files)

## Actionable Protocol

### Daily Non-Negotiables
- **Take 400–800 mcg of 5-MTHF (methylfolate)** — the active form, bypasses the broken enzyme
  - Brand examples: Thorne 5-MTHF, Jarrow 5-MTHF, Seeking Health Optimal Folate
  - Take in the morning with food
- **NEVER take regular folic acid** — it competes with 5-MTHF and can actually worsen outcomes in C677T individuals
  - Check all multivitamins and cereal products for "folic acid" — avoid or replace them
- **Eat methyl donors**: eggs (choline), liver (if ferritin < 100), leafy greens, beets
- **Take methylcobalamin (B12)** — methylation requires B12 as a co-factor. 500–1,000 mcg sublingually

### Blood Monitoring
- **Homocysteine**: Target < 7 µmol/L. Test every 6 months.
  - If > 10 µmol/L: increase 5-MTHF to 800–1,000 mcg + add B6 (P5P form) 25–50 mg
  - If > 15 µmol/L: consult a functional medicine doctor immediately
- **Serum folate**: Should be in normal-to-high range; if low, increase dose

### Foods High in Natural Folate (Preferred)
- Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale, arugula)
- Liver (1–2x/month if ferritin is optimized)
- Avocado
- Lentils and legumes
- Asparagus, broccoli, Brussels sprouts

### Avoid
- All products with "folic acid" on the label (common in breads, cereals, multivitamins)
- Alcohol — depletes methylation capacity significantly
- Excessive caffeine — can elevate homocysteine
