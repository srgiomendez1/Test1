## What This Gene Does

The TG gene encodes **thyroglobulin**, the main protein produced by the thyroid gland. Thyroglobulin is the backbone onto which iodine attaches to make thyroid hormones (T3 and T4). Variants in this gene don't directly break thyroid function — instead, they create a protein shape that the immune system is more likely to **misidentify as foreign** and attack.

This is the mechanism of **Hashimoto's thyroiditis** (autoimmune hypothyroidism).

## What the TG +/- Means for You

- **TG rs180223 +/-**: 10-fold increased risk of developing autoimmune thyroid disease
- **TG rs2076740 +/-**: Additional 2-fold risk
- The key point: **this is a triggered risk, not a certainty**. Hashimoto's only activates when inflammation is present — particularly gut inflammation. You control whether these genes get switched on.

## What Hashimoto's Does If Untreated

- Chronic fatigue and brain fog
- Weight gain, cold intolerance
- Depression, anxiety (connects to your HTR2A serotonin variants)
- Hair thinning, dry skin
- Joint pain (connects to your DIO2 + HLA joint variants)
- Eventual hypothyroidism requiring lifelong hormone replacement
- Compounded by your CDKAL1 gut variant — leaky gut is a known Hashimoto's trigger

## Actionable Protocol

### Blood Tests (Priority)
Order these now and repeat annually:
- **TSH** — Target: 0.5–1.5 mIU/L (note: this is tighter than the standard "normal" range of 0.4–4.5)
- **TPO antibodies (anti-TPO)** — Should be negative; elevation means autoimmunity is active
- **TGAb (anti-thyroglobulin antibodies)** — Should be negative
- **Free T4 and Free T3** — Assess actual thyroid hormone levels
- **Reverse T3** — Elevated rT3 indicates thyroid conversion problems

If antibodies are elevated or TSH > 2.0 with symptoms, consult an integrative/functional medicine physician about treatment.

### Dietary Interventions (Most Important)
- **Eliminate cow dairy** (MCM6 +/+ — gut inflammation → thyroid attacks)
- **Eliminate seed oils** (PON1/GSTP1 — systemic inflammation)
- **Eat selenium-rich foods** — Brazil nuts (1–2/day), sardines, oysters. Selenium is the most evidence-backed nutrient for reducing thyroid antibodies
- **Eat iodine sources** — Seafood 2x/week (salmon, sardines, shrimp). Iodine is required for thyroid hormone synthesis; deficiency accelerates Hashimoto's. Do not over-supplement iodine (> 1,000 mcg/day can worsen Hashimoto's)
- **Anti-inflammatory diet** — berries, olive oil, fatty fish, greens

### Supplements
- **Selenium** — 100–200 mcg/day of selenomethionine (not selenite)
  - Meta-analysis shows 12-month selenium reduces TPO antibodies significantly
- **Vitamin D3** — 2,000–5,000 IU/day (you already need this for CYP2R1 variant)
  - Vitamin D deficiency is a major Hashimoto's trigger; repleting D often reduces antibodies
- **Magnesium glycinate** — 200–400 mg at night; co-factor for thyroid hormone conversion
- **Zinc** — 15–30 mg/day (also benefits your FAM9B testosterone variant)

### Lifestyle
- **Reduce chronic stress** — cortisol directly suppresses thyroid function and activates autoimmunity
- **Prioritize sleep** — see ABCC9 sleep variant; poor sleep elevates inflammatory cytokines that trigger thyroid attacks
- **Fix gut health first** — leaky gut is a primary driver of all autoimmunity; your CDKAL1 variant is priority

### If Medical Treatment Is Needed
- Ask for **Armour Thyroid or NP Thyroid** (desiccated thyroid) rather than synthetic T4 only (Synthroid/levothyroxine). Your DIO2 variant impairs conversion of T4 to the active T3 — desiccated thyroid provides both.
