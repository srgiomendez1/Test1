## The 5 Non-Negotiables

1. **Take methylfolate (5-MTHF) 400–800 mcg daily** — MTHFR C677T +/+ is the single most important finding. Never take regular folic acid — it competes with 5-MTHF and worsens outcomes in homozygous carriers.
2. **Get baseline labs: Lp(a), homocysteine, Vitamin D, testosterone, full thyroid panel with TPO/TGAb** — Multiple variants require actively tracked targets.
3. **Eliminate cow dairy entirely** — MCM6 G/G = no lactase persistence = lactose intolerance + systemic inflammation from dairy. This is a hard rule, not a preference.
4. **Eliminate seed oils completely** — PON1 A/A (Met/Met, reduced activity) + GSTP1 Val/Val = your oxidized lipid defense is doubly impaired. Canola, sunflower, soybean, corn, and cottonseed oils are out.
5. **Active cardiovascular monitoring** — LPA G/G + CTH T/T + MTHFR C677T is a triple cardiovascular risk stack. Lp(a) and homocysteine must be checked and managed.

## Dangerous Variants — Requires Active Management

### MTHFR C677T (rs1801133) — A/A on +strand = T/T coding = Homozygous
**The most critical finding.** MTHFR converts folate to 5-MTHF (active form). The C677T +/+ variant reduces enzyme activity by ~70%, leaving only ~30% of normal function.

**Consequences:**
- Cannot convert folic acid or dietary folate efficiently
- Homocysteine accumulates (vascular + neurological toxin)
- Serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine synthesis impaired (methylation-dependent)
- DNA repair slowed
- Compounds both LPA and CTH cardiovascular risk

**Protocol:**
- 5-MTHF 400–800 mcg daily (morning with food)
- Methylcobalamin B12 500–1,000 mcg sublingual
- Riboflavin (B2) 10–20 mg as MTHFR cofactor
- Never folic acid (check all multivitamins and enriched grain products)
- Test homocysteine every 6 months, target < 7 µmol/L

---

### LPA (rs10455872) — G/G = Homozygous Risk
**Elevated Lipoprotein(a) — genetically fixed, not diet-responsive.**

Lp(a) is 80–90% genetically determined. G/G at rs10455872 drives significant Lp(a) elevation. Lp(a):
- Is pro-atherogenic (plaque formation) independent of LDL
- Is pro-thrombotic
- Is NOT lowered by statins (statins may raise Lp(a) slightly)
- Risk is compounded by CTH-driven homocysteine and CDKN2A/B

**Protocol:**
- Test Lp(a) every 6 months, target < 65 nmol/L (< 30 mg/dL)
- Niacinamide (B3) 500–1,500 mg/day (strongest natural Lp(a) reducer; 20–30% reduction)
- Vitamin C 1,000–2,000 mg/day (reduces oxidized Lp(a) + arterial inflammation)
- Omega-3 EPA+DHA 2–4 g/day
- Sauna 4–5x/week (20+ min)
- Discuss PCSK9 inhibitors or emerging therapies (Pelacarsen, Inclisiran) with cardiologist if Lp(a) > 125 nmol/L

---

### CTH (rs1021737) — T/T = Homozygous Risk
**Transsulfuration pathway impaired — compounds MTHFR.**

CTH converts homocysteine → cysteine → glutathione. T/T impairs this pathway. Combined with MTHFR C677T, both primary homocysteine clearance routes are blocked:
- Methylation route (MTHFR): ~70% impaired
- Transsulfuration route (CTH): impaired

Homocysteine elevation from this combination is near-certain without intervention.

**Protocol:**
- P5P (active B6) 25–50 mg/day (directly supports transsulfuration)
- Betaine/TMG 500–1,500 mg/day (independent methylation donor via BHMT pathway)
- Methylcobalamin B12 (co-factor for both pathways)
- 5-MTHF (already in MTHFR protocol — covers both)
- Target homocysteine < 7 µmol/L; > 15 requires immediate medical consult
- Eliminate alcohol (major homocysteine elevator)
- Alert dentist: avoid nitrous oxide (destroys B12)

---

### GSTP1 (rs1695) — G/G = Val105/Val105 = Homozygous
**Oxidized lipid and reactive oxygen species (ROS) detox impaired.**

GSTP1 (Glutathione S-Transferase Pi 1) neutralizes oxidized lipids and ROS products. Val/Val (G/G) has reduced catalytic efficiency compared to Ile/Ile. The "heavy metals" claim in the original AJ Consulting PDF was overstated — the evidence for heavy metal clearance via GSTP1 is mixed. The primary documented effect is impaired handling of oxidized lipids and electrophilic compounds.

**Note:** PON1 A/A (rs854560) is an independent and additive hit — PON1 normally protects HDL from oxidative damage. Having both Val/Val GSTP1 AND Met/Met PON1 means reduced oxidized lipid defense at two levels.

**Protocol:**
- Eliminate seed oils completely (primary trigger for oxidized lipid load)
- NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine) 600–900 mg/day (glutathione precursor)
- Cruciferous vegetables daily (sulforaphane upregulates NRF2 → compensatory antioxidant response)
- Alpha-lipoic acid (ALA) 300–600 mg/day (antioxidant recycling)
- Glutathione (liposomal) 500 mg/day as supplemental support

## Concerning Variants — Monitor and Manage

### SOD2 (rs4880) — A/A = Val16/Val16 = Homozygous Risk
Val/Val reduces mitochondrial antioxidant defense. This is NOT an iron dysregulation issue (the original report's iron-cardiomyopathy claim applies only to hereditary hemochromatosis, not SOD2). Effect: elevated mitochondrial oxidative stress, especially under high-intensity exercise.

**Protocol:** CoQ10 200–400 mg/day, Vitamin E (mixed tocopherols) 200 IU, magnesium glycinate (mitochondrial co-factor), sauna for mitochondrial hormesis.

---

### MTHFD1 (rs2236225) — +/+
Secondary folate enzyme. Compounds MTHFR. The active MTHFR methylfolate protocol covers this. No additional supplements required beyond the core methylation stack.

### DIO2 (rs12885300) — +/+ (possibly Thr92Ala homozygous)
DIO2 converts T4 (inactive) → free T3 (active thyroid hormone). Thr92Ala reduces local T3 production, particularly in cartilage, brain, and joint tissue. Can present as brain fog and joint pain even with "normal" TSH.

**Protocol:** Full thyroid panel with Free T3 (not just TSH), test every 6–12 months. Target Free T3 in upper third of range. Selenium (selenomethionine) 200 mcg/day supports iodothyronine deiodinase activity.

---

### HLA-DRB1 (rs660895) — A/A = Homozygous Shared Epitope
The HLA shared epitope is the most replicated RA genetic marker. A/A is a stronger signal than A/- (heterozygous). Also associated with Hashimoto's thyroiditis overlap. Combined with IL2RA +/+, the autoimmune risk is meaningful but inflammation-dependent — see autoimmune profile below.

---

### IL2RA (rs2104286) — T/T = Homozygous
IL2RA = interleukin-2 receptor alpha (CD25) on regulatory T-cells. T/T impairs regulatory T-cell suppression of aberrant immune responses. Together with HLA-DRB1, this constitutes the core autoimmune risk profile. Joint-focused (RA pattern) rather than systemic.

**Protocol:** Omega-3 EPA 3–4 g/day, Vitamin D3 4,000–5,000 IU/day (target 60–80 ng/mL serum), curcumin 500–1,000 mg/day with piperine.

### MCM6/LCT (rs4988235) — G/G = No Lactase Persistence
Neither of the two common lactase persistence alleles is present. Lactase production drops after weaning. This means dairy (lactose) causes inflammation and GI symptoms. This is a hard constraint.

**Key distinction:** This only affects lactose. Casein (milk protein) sensitivity is a separate, unrelated mechanism — the original PDF conflated these; that claim was removed.

**Rule:** Eliminate cow dairy. Goat dairy may be better tolerated (different casein structure, less lactose per volume) — test individually.

### FAM9B (rs5934505) — +/+
Associated with increased risk of low testosterone. This is a genetic predisposition, not a diagnosis.

**Protocol:** Free and total testosterone annually; target 500–1,500 ng/dL. Zinc 15–25 mg/day, Vitamin D (already in protocol), resistance training (testosterone-stimulating), sleep optimization (< 6 hours sleep directly suppresses testosterone).

### HTR2A + SLC6A4 — Serotonin Profile
- **HTR2A (rs6313) C/C:** Serotonin receptor hypersensitivity. Higher receptor binding affinity. Amplifies both positive and negative serotonin signaling.
- **SLC6A4 (+/+):** Short/short allele at serotonin transporter. Slower serotonin reuptake from synapses.

Together: serotonin stays in the synapse longer AND receptors are more sensitive to it. In a stable environment, this can mean deeper emotional experiences. Under stress or sleep deprivation, it creates vulnerability to anxiety and mood dysregulation.

See mental health profile below for full context.

### CDKAL1 (rs6908425) — C/C = Homozygous
GWAS association with Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, psoriasis, and Type 2 diabetes. NOT a "leaky gut" gene (the original PDF's framing was inaccurate — the mechanism is gut immune dysregulation, not intestinal permeability).

**Protocol:** Berries daily (anthocyanins are the most studied IBD anti-inflammatories), fermented foods, Omega-3 EPA+DHA, avoid NSAIDs routinely (worsen gut mucosa), keep fasting glucose < 85 mg/dL.

### PON1 L55M (rs854560) — A/A = Met55/Met55
PON1 circulates on HDL and neutralizes oxidized lipids before they damage arteries. The Met55 (A) allele reduces mRNA stability and enzyme secretion. A/A = reduced PON1 oxidized lipid defense.

**Note:** Sergio and Ashley have OPPOSITE genotypes here. Ashley is T/T (Leu/Leu = favorable). Sergio is A/A (Met/Met = reduced). This is confirmed and important — their PON1 status is diametrically different.

**Protocol:** Eliminate seed oils. Olive oil and saturated fats (butter from non-dairy or coconut) as primary cooking fats. This is partially overlapping with the GSTP1 protocol.

### CLU (rs11136000) — C/C = Homozygous Risk
CLU (clusterin) is a secreted extracellular chaperone protein that binds misfolded amyloid-beta peptides outside cells and routes them for clearance. C/C is associated with lower CLU expression → reduced extracellular amyloid clearance → elevated Alzheimer's risk.

**Important accuracy note:** The original PDF called CLU an "autophagy gene." This is mechanistically wrong. CLU is NOT an intracellular autophagy protein — it is an extracellular chaperone. Intermittent fasting still triggers intracellular autophagy (a beneficial independent process) but via mTOR/AMPK pathways, not through CLU.

**Protocol:** Sleep 7–9 hours (amyloid clearance via glymphatic system is sleep-dependent), intermittent fasting (16:8 triggers autophagy independently), berry consumption (blueberry anthocyanins slow amyloid plaque progression in studies), Omega-3 DHA (brain-specific DHA reduces amyloid deposition), BDNF-raising exercise (Zone 2 cardio).

### CDKN2A/2B — rs1333049 C/C (Triple Homozygous)
rs1333049 is located in ANRIL, a long non-coding RNA in the CDKN2A/B locus. C allele = ~20–30% increased coronary artery disease risk per allele. Sergio is C/C homozygous at all three linked SNPs in this region = cumulative CAD risk elevation. Effect is on coronary artery disease risk, not a cancer-specific variant.

**Protocol:** hsCRP < 1.0 mg/L (best practical target), flavonoid-rich diet (quercetin, kaempferol — blueberries, apples, green tea), statins may be relevant long-term — discuss with cardiologist given LPA + CDKN2 combination.

### CYP2A6 — Reduced Activity
Slow metabolizer of nicotine and certain mold toxins (aflatoxins, ochratoxin). Mold exposure has a more prolonged toxic effect in reduced-CYP2A6 individuals.

**Rule:** Avoid water-damaged buildings, check for visible mold. High-risk foods for mold mycotoxins: corn, peanuts, stored grains. Never smoke — nicotine detox is impaired.

---

### CYP1A2 (rs762551) — +/+ Slow Caffeine Metabolizer
CYP1A2 metabolizes caffeine. +/+ means caffeine has a longer half-life, increasing cardiovascular stress duration per cup.

**Rule:** Cut off caffeine before 11 AM. Limit to 1–2 cups/day. Avoid caffeine if experiencing anxiety or palpitations — the dose-response curve is steeper for slow metabolizers.

---

### Wnt16 (rs3779381) — +/+
Wnt16 is involved in bone density regulation, particularly cortical bone mass. +/+ is associated with lower peak bone density potential.

**Protocol:** Resistance training (mechanical loading is the strongest bone density stimulus), Vitamin D3 4,000 IU + K2 (MK-7) 100–200 mcg/day (calcium directs to bone not arteries), calcium from food not supplements (supplement calcium has mixed cardiovascular safety data).

### TG (rs180223) — C/C — *Downgraded from Dangerous*

**Protocol:** TPO antibodies + TGAb annually, selenium 150–200 mcg/day (selenomethionine — directly reduces thyroid antibody titers in clinical studies).

## Advantageous Variants — Leverage These

### ACTN3 (rs1815739) — T/T = X/X = No Functional Alpha-Actinin-3
ACTN3 R577X: the T allele introduces a stop codon. T/T means NO functional ACTN3 protein in fast-twitch fibers. Paradoxically, this is a clear **endurance advantage**:
- Muscles default to slow-twitch oxidative metabolism
- Higher mitochondrial density
- Superior lactate clearance
- Better sustained output over hours
- Elite distance runners, cyclists, and triathletes are disproportionately T/T

**Trade-off:** Explosive power and sprint speed are limited. Power sports (sprinting, Olympic lifting) will always feel harder than endurance. Focus training on what your genetics are built for: Zone 2 cardio, long-duration efforts.

---

### NRF2 — +/+
NRF2 is the master regulator of the antioxidant response element. Your variant upregulates NRF2 signaling, which increases production of glutathione, SOD, catalase, and other endogenous antioxidants.

**This partially compensates for GSTP1 and PON1 impairments.** NRF2 upregulation = more antioxidant capacity is made in response to oxidative stress.

**Activators (leverage these):** Sulforaphane from cruciferous vegetables, fasting, exercise, sauna, cold exposure.

---

### ADIPOQ (rs1501299) — +/+
ADIPOQ encodes adiponectin, the hormone released from fat cells during fasting that improves insulin sensitivity, fat oxidation, and anti-inflammatory signaling. Your +/+ variant means your adiponectin response is enhanced above average.

**Practical superpower:** Your body responds better to intermittent fasting than most people. Fasting periods trigger disproportionately higher adiponectin → better fat burning, better blood sugar control, stronger anti-inflammatory signal.

**Protocol:** 16:8 intermittent fasting is your ideal eating window. Your genetics reward it more than the average person.

---

### SCN9A — High Pain Tolerance *(Source: AJ Consulting PDF — not array-confirmed)*
SCN9A encodes the Nav1.7 sodium channel which controls pain signal transmission. Variants in this gene are strongly associated with altered pain sensitivity (both high and low tolerance phenotypes). The specific rs6746030 variant is NOT present on the AncestryDNA V2.0 chip — it was reported by AJ Consulting from a supplemental analysis. Cannot be confirmed from raw data.

**If accurate:** Above-average pain threshold; likely to underestimate injury severity; important not to "push through" pain signals, as they may already be attenuated.

## Mental Health Genetic Profile

Sergio's mental health genetic landscape has four converging inputs:

### 1. Methylation Deficiency → Neurotransmitter Depletion
MTHFR C677T +/+ with only ~30% MTHFR function means the methylation cycle (which builds serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine from amino acid precursors) is chronically underpowered. This is the root cause of neurotransmitter insufficiency — separate from receptor sensitivity.

**Signs:** Fatigue, low motivation, anhedonia, brain fog, difficulty concentrating. These symptoms are primarily methylation-driven, not personality or circumstance.

**Fix:** The 5-MTHF + methylcobalamin + B6 P5P protocol directly addresses this. Improvements typically begin within 2–4 weeks of starting methylated B vitamins.

### 2. HTR2A C/C — Serotonin Receptor Hypersensitivity
C/C means your serotonin receptors bind serotonin more avidly. Combined with SLC6A4 short/short (slower reuptake), serotonin dwells longer in the synapse at a receptor that is more sensitive to it. In a low-stress context this is fine. Under chronic stress, poor sleep, or methylation depletion, it amplifies anxiety and mood instability.

### 3. FAM9B +/+ — Low Testosterone Predisposition
Testosterone is a direct mood regulator in men (energy, confidence, motivation, libido). FAM9B +/+ creates a genetic drift toward lower testosterone. Combined with methylation depletion, the cumulative effect is low energy and flat affect that may not respond to standard psychiatric approaches.

**Fix:** Annual testosterone testing, resistance training, sleep optimization, zinc + Vitamin D in the supplement stack.

### 4. DIO2 +/+ — Thyroid-Brain Axis
Reduced T4→T3 conversion in brain tissue can present as cognitive fog, low motivation, and depression even when standard TSH is "normal." Free T3 in the lower third of range = suboptimal thyroid function in brain tissue.

**Fix:** Free T3 testing is essential (standard TSH panels will miss this). Target Free T3 in upper third of reference range.

**Profile summary:** This is a biochemical mental health profile, not a psychological one. The most effective interventions are nutritional and hormonal — methylated B vitamins, thyroid optimization, testosterone maintenance — rather than purely behavioral.
