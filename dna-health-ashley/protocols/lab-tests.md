## Full Thyroid Panel (Critical — DIO2 + HLA)

TSH alone will miss DIO2 impairment. Request every component:

| Test | Optimal Target | Why |
|------|---------------|-----|
| TSH | 0.5–2.0 mIU/L | Tighter than standard range; aim for lower half |
| Free T4 | Mid-to-upper normal | Thyroid output |
| **Free T3** | **Upper quartile of normal** | DIO2 T/T means this may be low despite normal T4 |
| Reverse T3 | < 15 ng/dL | High rT3 blocks T3 receptors |
| **TPO antibodies** | Negative | HLA shared epitope → Hashimoto's risk |
| **TGAb** | Negative | Thyroglobulin antibodies |

**If Free T3 is low-normal with normal TSH:** This is DIO2 impairment. Discuss T3-containing therapy with a functional medicine physician (desiccated thyroid or T3 supplement).

**If antibodies positive:** Autoimmune thyroid activity is confirmed. Intensify selenium, optimize gut health, consider GI consult.

## Hormonal Testing Specifics (Female)

For COMT A/G + MTHFR A1298C, estrogen metabolism testing is uniquely valuable:
- **Estradiol (E2)** — day 3 of cycle and day 21 (luteal); tracks estrogen levels and clearance
- **Progesterone** — day 21 for luteal adequacy
- **Estrogen metabolite ratio (2-OH/16-OH)** — if DUTCH test is available; shows whether estrogen is taking the safe (2-OH) or potentially harmful (16-OH) pathway. Your COMT + DIM supplementation directly shifts this ratio toward 2-OH.
- **DUTCH Complete test** — comprehensive 24-hour urine hormone panel that maps estrogen metabolism pathways; recommended if PMS, PMDD, or cycle irregularities are present

## How to Request These Tests

**From your primary care physician:**
- "I'd like a complete thyroid panel including Free T3, Reverse T3, TPO antibodies, and TGAb"
- "I'd like homocysteine, serum B12, RBC folate, and hsCRP"
- "I'd like an ANA screen, vitamin D, and a full iron panel including ferritin"

**Direct-to-consumer options:**
- Ulta Lab Tests — most affordable comprehensive panels
- LabCorp / Quest via PCP order
- DUTCH Test (hormones) — online order; home collection
