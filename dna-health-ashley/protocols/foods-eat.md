## Your Power Foods

### Eggs — MTHFR A1298C · COMT · MTRR
**Eat: 2–3 eggs daily**

Eggs are your single most important food for methylation:
- **Choline** (in yolk) is a direct methyl donor — essential when your MTHFR + MTRR create methylation deficit
- **Complete amino acids** including methionine (methylation substrate) and cysteine (glutathione precursor)
- **B12** in yolk — supports MTRR-impaired B12 recycling
- **PEMT connection:** Your PEMT rs7946 T/C (heterozygous) means partial impairment of internal choline synthesis — dietary choline from eggs is even more important

Pasture-raised preferred. Always eat the yolk.

### Berries — CDKAL1 · COMT · HTR2A · Brain
**Eat: ½–1 cup daily**

- **Blueberries:** Highest anthocyanins; gut anti-inflammation (CDKAL1 IBD risk); BDNF-boosting for brain (your BDNF is normal — leverage it)
- **Blackberries + raspberries:** High quercetin and ellagic acid; anti-inflammatory, gut microbiome support
- **Strawberries:** Vitamin C; antioxidant; collagen support for joints (HLA risk)

Frozen is equally effective. Add to first meal or eat as snack.

### Olive Oil (Extra Virgin) — Anti-Inflammatory
**Use: as primary cooking/dressing oil (2–3 tbsp/day)**

EVOO contains oleocanthal (a natural COX inhibitor, ibuprofen-like anti-inflammatory) directly relevant for your HLA joint and STAT4 autoimmune risk. It also supports PON1 activity and healthy HDL function — a bonus on top of your already-favorable PON1 genetics. Use it as your default fat in place of seed oils.

### Brazil Nuts — DIO2 Thyroid · HLA Thyroid Overlap
**Eat: 1–2 nuts per day (not more)**

2 Brazil nuts = ~150–200 mcg selenium. This:
- Reduces thyroid antibody titers (HLA/TG thyroid risk)
- Supports DIO2 T4→T3 conversion enzyme
- Supports glutathione peroxidase
**Do not exceed 3–4/day** — selenium toxicity risk.

### Fermented Foods — CDKAL1 Gut Microbiome
**Eat: daily small serving**

- **Goat or cow yogurt, kefir** — you CAN eat dairy (MCM6 A/G = lactase persistent); fermented dairy supports microbiome + provides calcium + B12
- **Kimchi, sauerkraut** — gut microbiome diversity directly reduces IBD flare susceptibility
- **Miso, tempeh** — fermented soy is safer than unfermented from an estrogen-receptor perspective (COMT)
- **Kombucha** — probiotic + organic acids; choose low-sugar versions

### Dark Chocolate (70%+) — HTR2A · CDKAL1 · Mood
**Eat: 1–2 squares daily**

Cacao epicatechin:
- Increases serotonin precursor availability (HTR2A support)
- Anti-inflammatory for CDKAL1
- Increases BDNF (healthy brain function — you already have good BDNF genetics)
- Mild mood elevation; stress cortisol reduction

70%+ cacao, no milk additives (read label).

---

### Beets — MTHFR · COMT · Methylation
**Eat: 2–3x/week**

Beets are the richest food source of betaine, a direct methyl donor that works via the BHMT pathway (independent of MTHFR). Especially useful for supplementing your impaired MTHFR + MTRR methylation.

## Meal Structure Template

### Breakfast (if eating breakfast)
- 2–3 eggs (choline, B12, protein)
- Avocado or olive oil drizzle
- Optional: small portion of berries
- Coffee before 11 AM

### Lunch / First Main Meal
- Protein: fish, chicken, meat, or eggs
- Large portion of cruciferous or leafy vegetables in EVOO
- Berries as dessert or alongside
- Fermented condiment (kimchi, kraut) on the side

### Dinner
- Fatty fish 3–4x/week; grass-fed meat other nights
- Cruciferous vegetable (nightly)
- Large salad with EVOO dressing
- Brazil nut (1–2) as dessert
- Small dark chocolate square
- Goat yogurt or kefir (dairy is fine for you)
