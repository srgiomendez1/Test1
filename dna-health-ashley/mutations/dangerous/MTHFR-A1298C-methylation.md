> **Important:** You do NOT have MTHFR C677T (G/G = clear). C677T is the more severe variant that reduces enzyme activity by ~70%. Your A1298C alone reduces activity by ~30–40%, which is manageable with the right supplements. The critical issue is that A1298C + MTHFD1 + MTRR together create a compound methylation deficit affecting multiple points in the same pathway.

---

## Understanding the Methylation Cycle

The methylation cycle converts homocysteine → methionine → SAM (S-adenosylmethionine), the body's universal methyl donor. SAM is used for:
- **Gene expression** — turning genes on/off via DNA methylation
- **Neurotransmitter synthesis** — producing serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine
- **Detoxification** — processing hormones, heavy metals, environmental toxins
- **Estrogen clearance** — metabolizing estrogen through phase II liver detox
- **DNA synthesis and repair**

### How Your Three Variants Stack

**MTHFR A1298C (+/+):** Reduces the enzyme that converts folate to 5-MTHF (active folate). Unlike C677T, A1298C primarily impairs the *biopterin recycling* side of MTHFR, which also affects neurotransmitter production (BH4 pathway). Estimated ~30–40% reduction in enzyme activity.

**MTHFD1 R653Q (+/+):** A second enzyme in the folate-one-carbon metabolism pathway. MTHFD1 interconverts one-carbon units needed for both DNA synthesis and the methylation cycle. Having both MTHFR A1298C and MTHFD1 (+/+) compounds the folate deficit.

**MTRR A66G (+/+):** Methionine synthase reductase — this enzyme *recharges* methionine synthase (MTR) by recycling B12. When MTRR is impaired, B12 gets "stuck" in an inactive form and MTR cannot keep converting homocysteine to methionine. This means your B12 needs to be consistently replenished in its active form (methylcobalamin).

**MTR A2756G (+/-):** Heterozygous impairment of methionine synthase itself (the enzyme that uses B12). Mild additional effect.

## What This Means Practically

- Your body produces **less SAM** than average → reduced methylation capacity system-wide
- **Estrogen clearance** is impaired → more sensitive to estrogen dominance; hormones may "pool" if methylfolate is low
- **Neurotransmitter synthesis** is slower → serotonin and dopamine production depends on BH4 (affected by A1298C), making your HTR2A serotonin hypersensitivity worse
- **Homocysteine may elevate** if methylfolate and B12 are not consistently supplemented (though you don't have CTH, so one clearance pathway is intact)
- **DNA repair** is reduced without adequate methylfolate

## Actionable Protocol

### Daily Non-Negotiables
- **5-MTHF (methylfolate) 400–800 mcg** every morning with food
  - Bypasses the broken MTHFR enzyme entirely
  - Brands: Thorne 5-MTHF, Seeking Health Optimal Folate, Jarrow 5-MTHF
  - Start at 400 mcg; go to 800 mcg if homocysteine is > 8 µmol/L
- **Methylcobalamin (B12) 1,000 mcg sublingual** daily
  - MTRR (+/+) means your body cannot recycle B12 efficiently — you must give it fresh active B12
  - Sublingual (dissolve under tongue) for superior absorption
  - Do NOT use cyanocobalamin — requires conversion your methylation system can't do well
- **NEVER take folic acid** — synthetic folic acid cannot be converted by your impaired MTHFR; it blocks the active form and worsens outcomes

### Additional Methylation Support
- **Betaine (TMG) 500–1,000 mg/day** — alternative methyl donor; bypasses MTHFR via the BHMT pathway; lowers homocysteine independently
- **P5P (Pyridoxal-5-Phosphate, active B6) 25–50 mg/day** — supports multiple points in the methylation and transsulfuration pathways
- **Riboflavin (B2) 25–50 mg/day** — MTHFR enzyme requires riboflavin as a cofactor; B2 supplementation can partially compensate for MTHFR impairment
- **Choline from food** — eggs (yolk) daily are critical; choline feeds the BHMT alternative methylation path

### Methylation-Rich Foods
- Eggs (daily — choline is a direct methyl donor)
- Leafy greens (spinach, kale, arugula) — natural folate
- Beets — betaine; directly supports methylation
- Liver (1–2x/month if ferritin is normal) — richest natural source of B12, B2, folate
- Avocado
- Legumes

### Things to Avoid
- **Folic acid** in any form (check multivitamins, enriched bread, cereals, protein bars)
- **Alcohol** — directly depletes methylation capacity
- **Oral contraceptives containing synthetic folate/folic acid** — if OCs are used, ensure they contain methylfolate or supplement separately
- **Excessive caffeine** — raises homocysteine in those with methylation issues

## Special Note for Women: Methylation and Hormones

Your MTHFR A1298C + MTRR combination is especially relevant for hormone health:
- Estrogen is metabolized in the liver via methylation (COMT pathway). Impaired methylation = slower estrogen clearance = tendency toward estrogen dominance
- This can manifest as: heavier or more painful periods, PMS, breast tenderness, mood shifts in the luteal phase
- Your COMT A/G (heterozygous) adds another layer — see COMT file
- **Fix:** Methylfolate + methylB12 + DIM or I3C (from cruciferous vegetables) to support Phase II estrogen metabolism

## Lab Monitoring

- **Homocysteine**: Target < 7 µmol/L. Test every 6 months.
  - If > 10 µmol/L: increase methylfolate to 800–1,000 mcg + add betaine
  - If > 15 µmol/L: consult functional medicine physician
- **Serum B12**: Target 400–900 pg/mL (many conventional labs allow too-low "normals")
- **RBC Folate**: Better than serum folate for assessing intracellular stores
- **Homocysteine response to supplementation**: Retest 3 months after starting methylfolate + B12
