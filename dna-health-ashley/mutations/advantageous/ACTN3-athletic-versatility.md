## What This Means

ACTN3 encodes alpha-actinin-3, a structural protein found exclusively in fast-twitch (Type IIb) muscle fibers — the ones responsible for power and speed. The R577X variant (rs1815739):
- **C allele = R577 (functional alpha-actinin-3)** — fast-twitch protein present
- **T allele = X577 (stop codon, no protein)** — no alpha-actinin-3

| Genotype | Alpha-actinin-3 | Profile |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| C/C (R/R) | Both copies present | Power/Sprint dominant |
| T/C (R/X = you) | One copy present | **Mixed — versatile** |
| T/T (X/X = Sergio) | None | Endurance dominant |

**You are R/X:** You have ONE functional copy of alpha-actinin-3. This gives you:
- **Fast-twitch capacity** present (unlike Sergio who has none)
- **Endurance capacity** also well-developed (T allele's endurance adaptation)
- **Most common genotype globally** — ~50% of people are R/X

## Practical Implications

- You benefit from **both strength/power and endurance training**
- You can gain muscle more effectively from lower rep ranges (4–6) than Sergio, while still responding well to higher rep endurance sets
- You are more physiologically suited to sports that require mixed demands: CrossFit, team sports, swimming, cycling with sprints

## Training Recommendations

- **Strength:** 4–8 reps for power development; 8–15 reps for hypertrophy — both work well
- **Cardio:** Zone 2 base (3x/week) + occasional high-intensity intervals (1x/week) — your fast-twitch capacity means you benefit from some sprint work
- **Progressive overload still applies** — your mixed profile means you adapt well to varied stimuli; don't just train one energy system
