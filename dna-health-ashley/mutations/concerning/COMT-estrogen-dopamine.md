## What COMT Does

COMT (catechol-O-methyltransferase) adds a methyl group to and thereby inactivates:
1. **Catecholamine neurotransmitters** — dopamine, norepinephrine, epinephrine
2. **Catecholestrogens** — metabolites of estrogen (2-OH-E1, 4-OH-E1) after Phase I liver detox

This makes COMT the primary enzyme responsible for:
- **Dopamine clearance in the prefrontal cortex** (working memory, stress response)
- **Estrogen metabolite clearance** (critical for hormone balance and cancer risk)

### The Methylation-COMT Connection

COMT requires SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) as its methyl donor. Your MTHFR A1298C + MTRR reduces SAM production. When SAM is low:
- COMT activity drops further (no methyl groups available to donate)
- Estrogen metabolites accumulate more
- Catecholamines clear more slowly → more stress reactivity

This is why methylfolate supplementation helps both your mood (HTR2A) and your hormone balance (COMT) simultaneously.

## Why This Matters for Women

**Estrogen metabolism pathway:**
1. Liver Phase I: Estrogen → catecholestrogens (via CYP1A2, CYP3A4)
2. Liver Phase II: Catecholestrogens → methylated, safe metabolites **(via COMT)**
3. Kidneys/bowel: Safe metabolites → excreted

If COMT is slow and SAM is low → catecholestrogen buildup → potential:
- PMS and PMDD intensification
- Breast tenderness
- Heavy or painful periods
- Heightened anxiety in luteal phase (days 14–28 of cycle)
- Longer-term: elevated estrogen metabolites are associated with breast health risk

### Combined with Your Other Variants
Your HTR2A C/C + MTHFR A1298C + COMT A/G creates a particular luteal-phase vulnerability:
- Progesterone rises after ovulation → drives serotonin fluctuations (HTR2A amplifies this)
- Estrogen metabolites are slower to clear (COMT + methylation impairment)
- **Result:** The week before your period may be disproportionately intense for mood, anxiety, and energy

## Protocol

### Estrogen Clearance Support
- **Methylfolate + methylB12 (non-negotiable)** — directly supports COMT by raising SAM
- **DIM (Diindolylmethane) 100–200 mg/day** — derived from cruciferous vegetables; shifts estrogen metabolism toward safer 2-OH-E1 pathway
- **Calcium D-Glucarate 500–1,000 mg** — inhibits beta-glucuronidase; prevents estrogen from being "un-conjugated" in the gut and re-circulating
- **Cruciferous vegetables daily** — broccoli, cauliflower, kale, Brussels sprouts (natural source of I3C/DIM)
- **Magnesium glycinate** — supports COMT and methylation

### Dopamine Balance
- **Tyrosine** (from protein — meat, eggs) — dopamine precursor; high-protein breakfast supports morning dopamine
- **Aerobic exercise** — directly increases dopamine AND COMT expression
- **Avoid excess caffeine** — amplifies dopamine/norepinephrine and worsens slow-clearance effect

### Tracking Your Cycle
Consider tracking mood, energy, anxiety, and sleep across your cycle to identify patterns. If symptoms cluster in days 14–28 (luteal phase), that's the COMT/HTR2A/methylation constellation at work. Targeted luteal-phase support:
- Increase magnesium glycinate to 400–500 mg in the luteal phase
- Add 5-HTP 50–100 mg in the evenings
- Reduce sugar and alcohol in the 10 days before your period
