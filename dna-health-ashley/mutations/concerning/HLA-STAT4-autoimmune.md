# 🟡 HLA-DRB1 + STAT4 + IL2RA — Autoimmune & Rheumatoid Arthritis Risk

> **Important context:** None of these variants diagnose RA or any autoimmune disease. They represent population-level risk associations. Having three converging variants means you have above-average genetic susceptibility — the actual risk is determined by lifestyle, environment, and infections. The intervention is the same regardless: aggressive anti-inflammation.

## What Each Variant Means

### HLA-DRB1 (rs660895 A/A)
The HLA region on chromosome 6 is the most important genetic determinant of autoimmune risk. rs660895 A/A is a tag SNP for the **HLA-DRB1 "shared epitope"** — the most strongly associated genetic marker for rheumatoid arthritis (OR 2–5x in homozygous carriers in some populations). The shared epitope is also linked to higher risk of Hashimoto's thyroiditis and other autoimmune conditions.

A/A (homozygous) has stronger association than A/- (heterozygous). This is your most significant autoimmune vulnerability.

### STAT4 (rs7574865 T/G)
STAT4 is a transcription factor that drives Th1 immune differentiation — essentially amplifying immune responses. The G (risk) allele is associated with:
- Rheumatoid arthritis
- Lupus (SLE)
- Sjögren's syndrome
- Type 1 diabetes
- Inflammatory bowel disease

T/G (heterozygous) = one risk copy. Effect is real but less than homozygous (G/G).

### IL2RA (rs2104286 T/T)
IL2RA encodes the interleukin-2 receptor alpha chain (CD25), expressed on regulatory T-cells. T/T is associated with impaired regulatory T-cell function → reduced ability to suppress autoimmune responses that have been triggered.

T/T + HLA risk + STAT4 = three converging signals toward autoimmune susceptibility.

## What This Means Practically

You are more susceptible than average to:
1. **Rheumatoid arthritis** — joint-specific (wrists, knuckles, symmetric)
2. **Autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto's)** — HLA shared epitope is also associated; see TG file
3. **General autoimmune reactivity** — infections, high-stress periods, or gut dysbiosis can trigger autoimmune flares more easily

The **actualization of this risk depends almost entirely on systemic inflammation levels**. Many people carry these genes and never develop autoimmune disease because they keep inflammation low. Your CDKAL1 and PON1 variants mean that dietary inflammation (seed oils, processed food) is a particularly strong trigger for you.

## Protocol

### Anti-Inflammatory Foundation (most critical)
- **Omega-3 EPA + DHA: 3–4 g/day** — strongest anti-inflammatory supplement evidence; directly suppresses TNF-α, IL-1, and IL-6 cytokines that drive RA; fish oil or algae oil
- **Curcumin (with piperine or liposomal) 500–1,000 mg/day** — NF-κB pathway suppression; specifically studied in RA; take with food and black pepper
- **EVOO 2–3 tbsp/day** — oleocanthal has ibuprofen-like COX inhibition without gut damage
- **Eliminate seed oils, processed food, excess sugar** — most impactful dietary intervention

### Specific Autoimmune Support
- **Vitamin D3 4,000–5,000 IU/day** — vitamin D is a direct immune modulator; D deficiency dramatically increases autoimmune risk; target 60–80 ng/mL serum level
- **Selenium (selenomethionine) 150–200 mcg/day** — reduces thyroid antibodies and immune hyperactivity; critical given HLA thyroid overlap
- **Probiotics (multi-strain)** — gut dysbiosis is a known trigger for autoimmune flares; Lactobacillus rhamnosus and L. casei specifically studied in RA
- **Green tea (EGCG)** — modulates Th17/Treg balance; 3 cups/day or EGCG 400–500 mg supplement

### Movement for Joints
- Joint mobility work daily (10 min circuit — hip circles, thoracic rotation, wrist/ankle mobility)
- Low-impact cardio preferred: swimming, cycling, rowing > running as primary cardio
- **Don't sit > 45 min without movement breaks** — joint inflammation is worsened by stasis

### When to See a Doctor
- Any **symmetric joint swelling** (especially small joints: knuckles, wrists) — get RF and anti-CCP tests immediately
- **Morning stiffness > 30 minutes** in joints
- Any joint symptoms + positive TPO/TGAb thyroid antibodies (Hashimoto's connection)
