## What This Means

Thyroglobulin (TG) is the protein backbone on which thyroid hormones T3 and T4 are synthesized. Variants in the TG gene have been studied as potential contributors to autoimmune thyroid disease (Hashimoto's), but the evidence is weaker than for HLA variants.

**Your higher thyroid concern is your HLA shared epitope** — not these TG variants. The TG variants are noted for completeness.

## Protocol

- Annual thyroid panel (TSH, Free T4, Free T3, rT3, TPO antibodies, TGAb)
- **Selenium (selenomethionine) 150–200 mcg/day** — the most evidence-backed nutrient for reducing thyroid antibody titers; reduces TPO antibody levels in randomized trials (Duntas et al., 2003)
- **Vitamin D3** — low D is consistently associated with Hashimoto's progression; maintain 60–80 ng/mL
- **Avoid high-dose iodine supplements** (> 500 mcg/day) — can trigger or worsen autoimmune thyroid flares
- Iodine FROM FOOD is fine and necessary: seafood 2–3x/week, seaweed/nori occasionally
